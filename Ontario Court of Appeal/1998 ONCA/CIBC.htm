                     ENDORSEMENT

[1]    The motions judge erred in refusing to grant the appellant
an adjournment in order to obtain an affidavit from Dr.
Pontarini, who had provided a letter of opinion relative to her
mental capacity at the time the mortgage was sought.


[2]    In the circumstances, the appellant was denied the
opportunity to produce evidence which may have raised a genuine
issue for trial in respect to her capacity to sign the mortgage,
with the result that, in our opinion, she did not have a fair
hearing.  There is no reason why the motions judge could not have
granted the appellant a brief adjournment to permit her to obtain
the affidavit.


[3]    Accordingly, the appeal is allowed and the judgment is set
aside.  However, this is not a case in which we would direct that
the action proceed to trial, rather, we order that the motion for
summary judgment be reheard before a different judge.


[4]    The appellant is granted leave to file an affidavit from
Dr. Pontarini incorporating what is contained in his report of
February 4, 1998 and any additional medical evidence, all of
which is to be filed by November 30, 1998.  The respondent is
entitled to file responding affidavit material and to conduct
cross-examinations, if so advised.  If the respondent does not
intend to proceed with a re-hearing of the motion, its counsel is
to so advise the appellant by October 23, 1998.


[5]    On this appeal, the appellant is to have costs of the
appeal book and factum.  The costs of the motion are reserved to
the judge rehearing the motion.
