                          ENDORSEMENT

[1]  The trial judge awarded judgment against the appellant for
unjust enrichment in amounts detailed in the judgment. Many of
the amounts allowed by the trial judge constitute no enrichment
whatever of the appellant. Of the items which were said to have
constituted enrichment, that is, construction of a septic system
- $818.00, the cost of basement construction - $15,000.00,
landscaping - $394.00, and $3,000 paid in cash to the appellant,
there is an issue as to whether there is a juristic reason for
the enrichment. The trial judge was of the view that because
there was no written contract between the parties, that indicated
the absence of a juristic reason. While there was no written
agreement, it is clear that there was some form of agreement
between the parties. The problem is that the evidence as to the
nature of that agreement was conflicting and the trial judge did
not resolve that conflict. In our view, there was a juristic
reason for the enrichment. Moreover, there also was no
deprivation since the respondent lived rent-free on the property
for a period of approximately 20 years. The balance of the items
allowed by the trial judge constituted no enrichment whatever of
the appellant. They were expenses of the respondent.


[2]  The appeal is allowed, the judgment of Thomson J. is set
aside and replaced by a judgment dismissing the action with
costs. The appellant is entitled to costs of the appeal.
