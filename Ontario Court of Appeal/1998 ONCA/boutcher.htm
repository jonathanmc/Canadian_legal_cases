                      ENDORSEMENT
                              

[1]  In our view there is no basis for interfering with this
conviction. The trial judge was well aware of the inconsistencies
in the complainant's evidence. Most of them were properly
characterized as minor and did not seriously affect the
complainant's credibility. It was open to the trial judge to find
that the statement by the appellant, when confronted by the
complainant's companion, was an admission of lack of consent.
Finally, it was open to the trial judge to take into account the
complainant's testimony as to her sexual preferences on the issue
of consent.


[2]  However, we are all of the view that the sentence cannot
stand. The trial judge erred in principle in placing undue
emphasis on the appellant's prior record. This related to
convictions entered after this offence was committed. In our view
a fit sentence is twelve months' imprisonment.


[3]  Accordingly, the appeal from conviction is dismissed, leave
to appeal sentence is granted, and the sentence is reduced to
twelve months' imprisonment.
