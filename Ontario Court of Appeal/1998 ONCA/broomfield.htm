                          ENDORSEMENT

[1]  In our view, the trial judge erred in instructing the jury
that Mr. Francis's evidence as to the date of the incident in
relation to the time he was at the appellant's home was based on
hearsay upon which they could not rely. This is conceded by
counsel for the respondent who submitted, however, that the error
was overborne by other portions of the charge. In our view, it
was not. Further, we think that the trial judge's
characterization of the witness Francis exceeded the bounds of
fair comment in such a way as to usurp the function of the jury.


[2]  The appeal is allowed, the conviction is set aside, and a
new trial is ordered.
