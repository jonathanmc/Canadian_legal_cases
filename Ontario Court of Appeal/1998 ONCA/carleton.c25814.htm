                     ENDORSEMENT


[1]    The appellant raises four grounds of appeal. The first and
main ground is that the trial judge failed to address the defence
of reasonableness. Although the trial judge did not expressly
deal with this defence, he implicitly considered it by his
treatment of the respondent's claim. In our view, the trial judge
accepted that the respondent acted reasonably in adopting Mr.
Otto's recommendation and that the recommendation to replace all
the siding was itself reasonable. We do not give effect to this
ground of appeal.


[2]    The appellant next submits that the trial judge erred in
admitting evidence of other claims against it. Even if the trial
judge should not have admitted such evidence, its admission did
not prejudice the appellant. There is uncontradicted evidence,
properly admissible, that the siding was inherently defective.
Mr. Otto's report discusses in detail the problems with the
siding that prompted his recommendation to replace it. Moreover,
the appellant itself introduced evidence of other claims. This
ground of appeal also fails.


[3]    The appellant's third submission is that the trial judge
erred in failing to hold that the respondent's action was barred
by the Limitations Act. We find no error in the trial judge's
treatment of the limitation defence.


[4]    Finally, the appellant submits that the trial judge erred
in holding the siding had a life expectancy of 25 to 30 years.
The appellant submits that the trial judge should have accepted
Mr. Newman's evidence of an industry-wide standard of a 20-year
life expectancy. The appellant's submission, if accepted, would
reduce the damages from 50 percent to 30 percent of the
replacement cost estimate. In finding a normal life expectancy of
25 to 30 years, the trial judge accepted the evidence of an
experienced property manager and the evidence of the architect
who prepared the respondent's expert report. We are not persuaded
that the trial judge acted unreasonably in accepting this
evidence. This final ground of appeal therefore fails.


[5]    For these reasons the appeal is dismissed with costs.
