                      ENDORSEMENT
                                
                                
[1]  Hoilett J. gave full and careful reasons in analysing the
principles of law applicable to limited retainers of solicitors.
He correctly concluded that Athe onus is on the solicitor who
seeks to limit the scope of his / her retainer and where there is
an ambiguity or doubt it will, generally, be resolved in favour
of the client@. On the evidence adduced before him, he found that
Mr. Comery=s retainer was limited to dealing with the complaint
that Mr. Coughlin had against his private insurer for having
terminated the payments he had been receiving pursuant to the
terms of a policy of disability insurance. Mr. Comery=s retainer
was thus limited to the assertion of Mr. Coughlin=s claim against
Mutual of Omaha rather than to an exploration of all of the
circumstances of the accident at the Algonquin Tavern almost four
years before.


[2]  Hoilett J. expressly accepted as Amore reasonable@ the
opinions of the experts called on Mr. Comery=s behalf regarding
the standard of his professional obligation in such circumstances
and accepted their evidence that Mr. Comery=s conduct was in
accordance with the standard to which they testified. In our
assessment, there was evidence on which the trial judge could
appropriately make those findings and reach those conclusions,
and this court cannot appropriately interfere with them:
Hodgkinson v. Sims, [1994] 3 S.C.R. 377 at 425-426.


[3]  The findings and conclusions referred to are decisive in
concluding this appeal against the appellant and, accordingly,
the appeal is dismissed with costs. There is no reason to hear
the cross-appeal and it is also dismissed, but without costs.
