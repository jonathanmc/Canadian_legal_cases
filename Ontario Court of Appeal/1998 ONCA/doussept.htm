                      ENDORSEMENT



[1]  This appeal, which was ably argued by both counsel, concerns
the motions judge’s interpretation of the indemnification
provisions of an agreement between the appellant and respondent.


[2]  We accept that indemnification from claims based upon the
respondent’s own negligence is not expressly dealt with in the
indemnification clause in issue. However, that does not end the
matter. In our view, the indemnification clause should be given a
reasonable, commercially sensible interpretation consistent with
the language that the parties chose to use. Every part of the
clause, including the words “... against all claims, including
claims made by the contractor’s personnel under Workers’
Compensation legislation ...” should be given some reasonable
contextual meaning. When that is done, we think that the clause,
by necessary implication, covers the subrogated WCB negligence
claim asserted here.


[3]  We thus agree with the motions judge’s conclusion on the
issue of the scope of the indemnification clause. The appeal is,
therefore, dismissed with costs.
