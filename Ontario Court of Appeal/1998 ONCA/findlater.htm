                      ENDORSEMENT


[1]  In our view, the motions court judge erred in assuming that
the defence of qualified privilege was “automatically raised” and
applicable. This is an issue that should be dealt with at trial
where all of the circumstances surrounding the complaint and its
investigation can be fully developed.


[2]  With respect to the tort of the intentional infliction of
nervous shock, we think that the statement of claim, looked at
broadly, does not support this claim. We thus agree with the
motions judge’s conclusion on this issue. It seems to us to be
clear that since the motions judge dismissed this claim as
against the individual defendants and since the company’s
liability, if any, is vicarious, the claim for this tort as
against the company must also be dismissed.


[3]  An order will issue in accordance with this endorsement.
