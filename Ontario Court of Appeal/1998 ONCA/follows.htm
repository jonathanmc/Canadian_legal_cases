                     ENDORSEMENT

[1]     The appellant asked this court to decline to exercise its
jurisdiction  on  the ground that the child's welfare  should  be
determined  by  the  Cayman  court.   We  did  not  call  on  the
respondent on this issue.  We are satisfied that Speigel  J.  and
Benotto   J. had jurisdiction to make the orders that  are  under
appeal.   Similarly,  this  court has jurisdiction  to  determine
whether the finding of contempt should be upheld.


[2]     Thus  the main question before us is whether  Benotto  J.
should  have  made the order that she did.  This question  raises
two   separate   issues.   First,  was  the  appellant   accorded
procedural   fairness;   that  is,  was  she   given   reasonable
particulars  of  the  allegations of contempt;  and  second,  was
Benotto  J.'s  finding of contempt reasonably  supported  by  the
evidence?


[3]     On the first issue we recognize that contempt motions  in
civil  proceedings have a criminal character.  This is especially
so  in this case because the contempt motion seeks to punish past
misconduct.   Therefore  the requirement that  the  appellant  be
given reasonable particulars is important. Although it would have
been  preferable for the particulars of the breaches  of  Justice
Beaulieu's order to have been set out in the notice of motion, we
are  satisfied  that  the  allegations in  paragraph  16  of  the
respondent's  affidavit, in a section of  that  affidavit  titled
"George  and Regina's contempt of Justice Beaulieu's order"  gave
the  appellant  fair  notice  of  the  allegations  against  her.
Similarly,  she had fair notice of the allegation  that  she  had
breached  Justice Speigel's order.  No objection to the  lack  of
or  inadequacy of particulars was raised before Benotto  J.   Nor
did  the  appellant  argue that she had been  prejudiced  by  the
conduct of the proceedings before Benotto J.


[4]     On  the second issue, we are satisfied that the  evidence
reasonably  supported  Benotto J.'s finding  "that  the  mother's
actions  in  continually denying and frustrating  access  are  in
contempt of the judgment of Beaulieu J. and the order of  Speigel J."


[5]     For  these  brief reasons the appeal  is  dismissed  with
costs.
