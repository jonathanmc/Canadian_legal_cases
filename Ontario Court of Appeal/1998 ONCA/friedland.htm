                     ENDORSEMENT

[1]  We do not agree that s . 4(3) of the State Immunity Act (the
Act) impliedly requires that any claim of immunity under the  Act
must  be fully litigated before the action in which the claim  is
made  can  proceed.  The section does no  more  than  provide  an
exception  to  the  waiver of immunity by  participation  in  the
proceedings provided for in s. 4(2) of the Act.


[2]  The appellant's obligation to deliver a statement of defence
and  otherwise  defend in a timely fashion are  governed  by  the
generally applicable procedural laws: see s. 17 of the  Act.  Any
relief  from those obligations pending appeal must come under  s.
134(2) of the Courts of Justice Act.


[3]   In  so  far  as  the application of the  United  States  is
concerned, we see no basis upon which we can interfere  with  the
order  of  Justice  Carthy. He properly  addressed  the  relevant
considerations.  The  appeal from  that  part  of  his  order  is
dismissed with costs.


[4]    With  respect  to  the  motion  brought  by  the  personal
defendants,  we  think a distinction must be  drawn  between  the
position  of  the United States and the position of the  personal
defendants. The United States commenced the proceedings in  which
the  counterclaim against it is brought. The personal  defendants
did  not  initiate any proceedings in this province and  must  be
seen  as  being  in  the position of "true"  defendants  claiming
immunity in proceedings initiated against them by the respondent.
In  those  circumstances,  we think the  balance  of  convenience
favours  awaiting the determination of the appeal in  March  1999
before  the personal defendants are required to defend  in  their
personal  capacity  the action brought against  them  by  way  of
counterclaim.


[5]   The  order  of Carthy J.A. as it applies  to  the  personal
defendants  will be varied accordingly. Costs of this motion  are
left to the court hearing the appeal.
