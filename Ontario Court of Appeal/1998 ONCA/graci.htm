                      ENDORSEMENT
                                
                                
[1]  The appellant, Richard Fletcher, pleaded guilty in 1994 in
the Provincial Court to a reduced charge of assault on his
brother Edward Fletcher and was fined and placed on 18 months=
probation. The agreed upon facts read to the court were as
follows:
                    As they were passing in a hallway,
          an altercation arose where Edward Fletcher
          was knocked backwards into the filing
          cabinets by Richard Fletcher. Richard
          Fletcher struck Edward Fletcher a number of
          times in the face and body. Edward Fletcher
          suffered bruises to the chin, temple, head
          and also a cut to his lips.
          
[2]  In these circumstances, and having regard to the evidence
that he heard at the trial in these proceedings, the trial judge
was more than justified in making the findings as to liability
that he did.


[3]  In this court, the appellant complains that the trial judge
did not make findings of credibility with respect to the issue of
provocation. The issue of provocation was not pleaded in the
civil proceedings.


[4]  With respect to the award of damages, very little was said
in this court on the subject, but in any event, having read the
material, we are not in a position to disagree with the award
made by the trial judge.


[5]  Accordingly, the appeal is dismissed with costs.
