                      ENDORSEMENT
                                

[1]   There  is  no doubt that the judgments for recovery  cannot
stand  for failure to comply with Rule 19.01(2). However, in  our
view  there  is a more fundamental difficulty. The uncontradicted
affidavit  evidence  of  Ms.  Fracassi,  the  principal  of   the
appellant, is that she was involved in a difficult pregnancy  and
then concerned with the health of her newborn child in the months
leading  up to the trial date and therefore unable to  assist  in
the  preparation of the case. Unfortunately, this information was
not  brought to the attention of the motions judge. In our  view,
it  is  in  the interests of justice to admit the fresh evidence,
set  aside  the judgments, set aside the order striking  out  the
statements  of  defence,  and dismiss  the  respondents'  motions
without costs.


[2]   At  least  some  fault  for what  occurred  lies  with  the
appellant  and in the circumstances we would not make  any  order
for costs of these appeals.
