                          ENDORSEMENT
[1]  The trial judge held that the intention of the parties was
clear. The appellant wanted a three and a half year guaranteed
service contract and, although the respondent was reluctant in
agreeing to this, the appellant eventually won and the board of
directors of the respondent corporation agreed to the guaranteed
term. The contract should be read so as to give effect to the
clear intention of the parties.


[2]  In our view, the trial judge erred in finding that clause 6
of the contract was repugnant to clause 12 and that clause 12 was
the operative clause. Such an interpretation does not give effect
to the intention of the parties but rather defeats it.


[3]  In our view there is no repugnancy between the two clauses.
Clause 12 gave the respondent the right to terminate the
agreement prior to the expiry of its term as set out in clause
two. This early termination then simply triggered the application
of the latter part of clause 6 which clearly provided that the
compensation continued to be payable for the full term even in
the event of early termination.


[4]  For these reasons, the appeal is allowed, the judgment is
set aside and, in its place, there will be judgment for the
appellant as endorsed on the appeal book.
