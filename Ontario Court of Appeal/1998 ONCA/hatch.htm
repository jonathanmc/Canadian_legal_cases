                      ENDORSEMENT
                               

[1]  Notwithstanding Ms. Secord's able and thorough submissions,
we are not satisfied that there is any basis for interfering with
the trial judge's finding that Dual Removal was liable on the
contract with the respondent. This was principally a question of
fact. The respondent was retained in a meeting with Mr. Abrams on
November 17, 1989 to decommission the site and construct a
transfer station for Dual Removal. Thus, it was open to the trial
judge to find that until February 1990 the contracts were with
Dual Removal. The fact that following February 1990 the
respondent was instructed to invoice Dual Transfer did not alter
the contractual relationship between the parties. Even after
February 1990, Dual Transfer had no assets and the project
continued to be directed by the principals and employees of Dual
Removal. Mr. Abrams himself conceded that Dual Transfer never
carried on business of any kind and the payments to the
respondent, both before and after February 1990, were made by the
same corporate entity.


[2]  The letter of January 24, 1992 confirms Dual Removal's
continuing liability for the respondents' fees. The trial judge
rejected Mr. Abrams' attempt to explain that letter. This
evidence gave further support for the trial judge's findings of
Dual Removal's liability.


[3]  We reach a similar conclusion with respect to Mr. Abrams'
personal liability. The trial judge found that at the
September 5, 1991 meeting Mr. Abrams gave a personal guarantee
for payment of the respondent's account. In view of the
relationship between the parties, it was open to the trial judge
to find that the terms of the guarantee had been agreed and were
sufficiently clear and unambiguous. As a result of the meeting
the respondent returned to work. Mr. Abrams never denied giving
the guarantee until the litigation commenced. Indeed he
acknowledged receiving the written guarantee and asked for a
change as to its form but not its substance.


[4]  It was not disputed that estoppel is available to prevent
Mr. Abrams from relying on the defence in the Statute of Frauds.
We do not accept that Mr. Abrams at any time withdrew the
guarantee. The only conduct pointed to by Ms. Secord is
Mr. Abrams' failure to return the written guarantee to the
respondents. That was not itself sufficient to constitute
withdrawal of the guarantee given at the September 5, 1991
meeting so as to defeat the operation of the equitable doctrine
of estoppel. Mr. Abrams never communicated to the respondent that
he was withdrawing his personal guarantee.


[5]  Finally, we do not agree that Mr. Abrams is liable only for
the fees and expenses incurred after the September 5, 1991
meeting. The guarantee covered all of the indebtedness to the
respondent.


[6]  The appeal is dismissed with costs.


[7]  We see no merit in the cross-appeal and the cross-appeal is
dismissed with costs.
