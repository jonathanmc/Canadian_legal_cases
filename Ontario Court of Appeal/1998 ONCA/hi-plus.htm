                          ENDORSEMENT

[1]  The appellant, Giovanni Boscarino, appeals from a judgment
in the amount of $190,095, against him personally, for monies
owing under a Lease entered into between Boscarino's Holdings
Inc. and the respondent. The trial judge held the appellant
personally liable on the basis of an indemnity clause contained
in an Offer to Lease pursuant to which the appellant agreed to
indemnify the respondent with respect to the obligations of
Boscarino's Holdings Inc. therein. The appellant also agreed to
sign the Lease as "guarantor and indemnifier". The Lease, entered
into approximately 42 months later between Boscarino's Holdings
Inc. and the respondent, was not signed by the appellant in his
personal capacity and did not contain any guarantee or indemnity
clause.


[2]  The appellant raises two main grounds of appeal. First, he
contends that the trial judge erred in failing to find that the
Lease had been entered into as a result of misrepresentations
made by Mr. Haghani, on behalf of the respondent, and that these
misrepresentations had resulted in a loss to Boscarino's Holdings
Inc. Second, he argues that the trial judge erred in finding him
responsible as indemnifier under the terms of the Offer to Lease
entered into between the parties when the Lease contained no such
clause.


[3]  The trial judge did not accept the appellant's evidence with
respect to the alleged misrepresentations but, rather, preferred
the evidence of Mr. Haghani. The appellant has not satisfied us
that the trial judge misapprehended the evidence on this issue.
It was open to the trial judge to find Mr. Haghani's evidence
more probable, as he did. The trial judge's conclusions on this
issue are supported by the evidence and there is no basis upon
which this court can interfere.


[4]  Further, it is our view that the trial judge was correct in
finding that the indemnity signed by the appellant in the Offer
to Lease was a valid collateral agreement that did not merge into
the subsequent Lease. First, the Lease was entered into between
different parties, namely Boscarino's Holdings Inc. and the
respondent. Although the appellant signed the Lease on behalf of
Boscarino's Holdings Inc., he was not a party to it. Therefore
the appellant can neither claim the benefit of the "no collateral
agreement clause" in the Lease, nor rely on the doctrine of
merger as between the respondent and himself. Second, even to the
extent that the doctrine of merger may be applicable, the
question is ultimately one of intention. The trial judge's
finding that the parties did not intend that the indemnity should
merge into the Lease is well supported by the evidence.


[5]  Consequently, the appeal is dismissed. As both parties
appear in person, there will be no order as to costs.
