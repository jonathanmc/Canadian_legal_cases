                     ENDORSEMENT

[1]     The  respondent was liable as a party to  trafficking  in
cocaine  under  s.21(1)(b) of the Criminal Code.   She  was  also
liable  as  a  principal  as a trafficker  under  s.4(1)  of  the
Narcotic  Control Act.  This is not a case where  the  respondent
merely  played  the  role  of  an intermediary  by  bringing  the
purchaser together with the potential seller.  Even if it can  be
said  that the respondent was an agent for the purchaser, if  she
did  one  or  more of the acts constituting trafficking,  as  set
forth  in  s.2 of the Narcotic Control Act, she is  guilty  as  a
principal.   As  the  respondent transported  and  delivered  the
narcotic  to the purchaser, she is accordingly guilty  under  s.2
and s.4(1) of the Narcotic Control Act.  As well, by facilitating
a  sale, she aided the seller and in that way she was guilty as a
party under s.21(1)(b).


[2]    On the facts as found by the trial judge, the respondent's
actions  are  not,  as  described in R. v. Greyeyes  (1997),  116
C.C.C. (3d) 334 (S.C.C.), "the acts of a mere purchaser" but  are
those  of a party to the offence of trafficking, as well as those
of an actual trafficker.


[3]     Accordingly, the appeal is allowed and the  acquittal  is
set  aside  and  a  conviction  is entered.   The  proceeding  is
remitted  to  the  trial  court which is  directed  to  impose  a
sentence that is warranted by law.
