                      E N D O R S E M E N T

[1]  The appellant raises three issues, all going to the weight
that the motions judge gave to particular factors relevant to the
decision whether the respondents’ undertaking as to damages ought
to be enforced. In our view, notwithstanding Mr. Clarke’s able
submissions, the motions judge acted within the broad ambit of
the discretion open to him. We think that he exercised his
discretion on a reasonable basis. He was entitled to conclude
that there were special circumstances militating in favour of a
decision that the undertaking as to damages should not be
enforced in the circumstances. Since the motions judge did not
err in principle in exercising his discretion, there is no basis
upon which to interfere with his decision.


[2]  In reaching this conclusion we do not wish to be taken to
have departed from the general rule that undertakings as to
payment of damages given at the time of an application for an
interim injunction, and made part of an interlocutory injunction
order, will be enforced.


[3]  The appeal is dismissed with costs.
