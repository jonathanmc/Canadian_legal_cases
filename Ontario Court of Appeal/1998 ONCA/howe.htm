                          ENDORSEMENT
[1]  The only issue argued on this appeal was the question of
whether the lease of the property by the vendor/plaintiff was
accompanied by "actual possession" within the meaning of s. 70(2)
of the Registry Act R.S.O. 1990, c. R.20. We agree with the trial
judge that in the unusual facts of this case the lessee was not
in actual possession. It had sublet the property to the landlord
who was the party in actual possession.


[2]  The assessment of damages was not argued orally, but left to
the panel to decide on the basis of the factums. We would not
interfere with the trial judge's assessment.


[3]  The appeal is dismissed with costs.
