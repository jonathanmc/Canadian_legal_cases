                      E N D O R S E M E N T
                                
                                
[1]  The circumstances that give rise to this appeal are most
unfortunate. In the first place, issue is taken with the reasons
of the trial judge as to whether the parties had agreed to expand
the issues directed to be tried by Weekes J. which related to an
October 22, 1991, will to encompass what has been described as a
Astore form@ will of August 1991, a 1985 will which had been
lost, a 1982 will, and an inter vivos transfer to the respondent
of the Treeview Drive property in August of 1991. We are not
satisfied that the conduct of the parties in conducting the
hearing amounted to an enlargement of the issues that were to be
determined. Certainly, they are inextricably intertwined and
resolving them in other than a single proceeding would make no
sense at all.


[2]  As to the merits of the issue whether the deceased had
approved and understood the contents of the October 22, 1991 will
and whether it was procured by undue influence, we acknowledge
some inconsistencies in the trial judge=s reasons, but her
ultimate findings, at least as to the testatrix=s lack of
understanding and approval of the will=s contents, are supported
by the evidence. This leaves only one concern and that is whether
an earlier 1982 will, which was not challenged, was revoked by
the two 1991 wills which the trial judge was not prepared to
uphold as valid. Once again, the record does raise concern but it
is apparent that the missing 1985 will, which the trial judge
found as a fact to have been drafted by a Polish speaking lawyer
and properly executed, would have the effect of revoking the 1982
will.


[3]  The result is an intestacy. This is consistent with the
result which the trial judge found was desired by the testatrix.
Her sons would be treated equally. Accordingly, in the unusual
circumstances of this case, we are not prepared to interfere with
the judgment below. The appeal is dismissed with costs.
