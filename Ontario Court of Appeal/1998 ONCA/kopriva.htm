                          ENDORSEMENT

[1]  The trial judge very carefully analyzed the question of
fiduciary duty and a possible breach of that duty. He found that
there was a fiduciary relationship between the parties on the
facts of the case, but that there was no breach of that duty by
the appellants. There was more than adequate evidence to support
both of those findings, and we would not interfere with them.


[2]  The trial judge did find, however, that the appellants were
negligent in carrying out their contractual obligations to the
respondents. Unfortunately, his reasons, on pages 16 and 17,
indicate that the trial judge based this portion of his decision
on the fact that Merrill Lynch failed to obtain an independent
appraisal of the real property involved. There was no expert
evidence at trial of the normal practice of persons or
corporations acting as sales agents of securities (as Merrill
Lynch did in this case) with respect to obtaining appraisals.
There was a prospectus which was delivered to the respondents
which clearly set out the acquisition price of the property,
information as to the tenants, and other details. There was no
evidence that any of this information was false or misleading in
any way. In our view, without the assistance of expert evidence,
in requiring an independent appraisal of real property to be done
in the circumstances of this case, the court would be imposing an
unrealistically high standard of investigation on persons in the
position of the appellants.


[3]  This was one of the many unfortunate situations where
investors were caught in the downturn in the real property
market. Although there were additional alleged derelictions of
duty on the part of the appellant Beaver, relied on by the
respondents, in our view, they do not raise the facts to the
level of a failure to meet the standard of care required in the
circumstances of this case.


[4]  The appeal is allowed and the cross-appeal is dismissed. The
judgment below is set aside, and a judgment will be entered
dismissing the action with costs. The appellants are entitled to
costs of the appeal.
