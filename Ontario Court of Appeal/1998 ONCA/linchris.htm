                     ENDORSEMENT
[1]    The defendant, Construction Ravin Boise Inc., ("Ravin
Boise") moves for  security for costs and for an order expediting
the appeal by Linchris Homes Limited from the summary judgment
pronounced by Chadwick J. on July 3, 1998.  The hearing of the
appeal has since been set for March 3, 1999; accordingly, that
aspect of the motion may be ignored.


[2]    The motion for security for costs is put upon the basis
that the appellant is a corporation, that there is good reason to
believe its appeal is frivolous and vexatious and that there is
good reason to believe that it has insufficient assets in Ontario
to pay the costs of the appeal.


[3]    There is no doubt about the plaintiff's corporate status
at the present time.  It is worth noting, however, that it was
dissolved on July 26, 1993 and was revived only for the purpose
of dealing with this litigation, on February 17, 1998.


[4]    The litigation arose out of a real estate transaction.
The plaintiff agreed to buy land from Ravin Boise in 1988 for
$3,100,000.  The sale did not close.  The plaintiff commenced an
action in May 1998 for the return of its deposit of $125,000.
Ravin Boise counterclaimed for substantial damages.


[5]    In 1998, Ravin Boise abandoned its claim for all but the
deposit and moved for summary judgment dismissing the plaintiff's
action and allowing Ravin Boise's claim for the deposit.  This
motion was successful in both respects.  The plaintiff now
appeals.


[6]    Keith McDowell, the principal of the plaintiff, was cross-
examined on June 11, 1998.  In the absence of financial
statements which he undertook to produce (but failed to do so),
he said that he did not believe that those financial statements
would show any activity in the company in the last four years.
Asked whether there were any assets in the company, whether it
owned any land, he responded "Not to my knowledge".  He also
indicated that the company had neither income nor debts.


[7]    An affidavit sworn by Mr. McDowell on October 26, 1998
indicates that the company "...has, however, in fact, carried on
business continuously and presently holds interests in a number
of properties all as set out in Schedule A hereto".  Schedule A
lists nine properties but provides no information as to the
nature of the interest held by the plaintiff in any of them.
There is no indication that the plaintiff has any equity.


[8]    I therefore conclude that there is good reason to believe
that the appellant has insufficient assets in Ontario to pay the
costs in issue.


[9]    The costs of the action were fixed by Chadwick J. at
$5,000 plus GST.  Ravin Boise estimates its costs of the appeal
at $11,834.20 including GST.  No issue was taken with this
figure.


[10]   An order will therefore go that on or before December 1,
1998 the appellant pay into court the sum of $17,000 to stand as
security for the costs of Ravin Boise in these proceedings.


[11]   The appellant will also pay to Ravin Boise its costs of
this motion.
