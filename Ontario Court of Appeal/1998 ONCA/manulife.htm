                             ENDORSEMENT
[1]  In our view, the summary judgment on the counterclaim for
punitive damages in the amount of $70,875 cannot stand on the simple
ground that the bank had not been served with a notice of motion
claiming such relief and, further, it does not appear that the notice
of motion seeking the amendment of the pleadings was "converted" into
one seeking such a judgment. The transcript of the hearing confirms
that the bank was not given proper notice of this particular claim. It
is not necessary to decide whether, on the materials before him, or
otherwise, it was open to the judge to grant a summary judgment for
punitive damages in that amount or in any other amount.


[2]  This appeal is allowed, with costs, and paragraphs 2 and 3 of
this judgment are set aside.


[3]  It was reasonably open to the judge to make the findings that he
did on the application to discharge the mortgages and we are not
persuaded that he erred in arriving at his ultimate conclusions.
Specifically, it was open to him to make the following finding:

          ... I am satisfied that Mr. Kelly thought that all
          he owed at the time of the mortgage statement was
          the amount shown on the mortgage statement itself.
          He did not think of the letter of credit as money
          owing because it had not become a debt to be paid.
          I would conclude that the person providing the
          statement from the bank no doubt had that same
          conclusion and had a reconsideration of that later
          on. The actual money that was outstanding on the
          mortgages at the time was in the neighbourhood of
          the $350,000. I think $355,537.90 was what was
          actually paid.

[4]  The appeal from this judgment is dismissed, with costs.
