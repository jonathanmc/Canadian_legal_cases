                      ENDORSEMENT

[1]  The respondents who are mother and sister of Mr. Magyar
sought an order before trial separating certain property issues
with which they are concerned from the issues of divorce, spousal
support and child support and the Family Law equalization
payment. In our view, the respondents were entitled to this
relief.


[2]  The respondents' motion, brought before trial, was adjourned
by the motions judge to the trial judge. The trial judge did more
than  the  respondents  asked  him  to  do.  He  struck  out  the
appellant's claim against the husband's mother and sister.


[3]  We think that he erred in making this order. In our view,
the property claims should be dealt with, but in a separate
proceeding before the spousal issues, including the required
equalization payment, are determined.


[4]  There will, therefore, be an order directing the trial of an
issue of the ownership of the two properties referred to in the
material. The appellant will be the applicant. The record will
include the pleadings in the divorce proceeding and any other
material that the parties may file within 14 days. The hearing of
the property issues should be expedited. No costs here or below.
