                      ENDORSEMENT
                                

[1]  While the motions judge did not use the language of Hunt v.
Carey Canada Inc., we are satisfied that in substance she applied
the "plain and obvious" test to the motion before her.


[2]  In our view, the motions judge did not err in finding that
the claim fails to disclose an absence of reasonable cause for
the police to proceed to charge the plaintiff. This is fatal to
the claims for malicious prosecution and negligence. As to the
Charter claims, there is no pleading of facts which would found a
breach of either s. 10 or s. 15.


[3]  The appeal must therefore be dismissed.
