                          ENDORSEMENT

[1]  In our view, this appeal must be allowed.


[2]  After a trial that proceeded during the months of November
1993 and September 1994, and written arguments of counsel which
were not submitted until the summer of 1996, the trial judge
delivered a 5-page set of reasons for judgment which were
essentially conclusory, in which he failed to make findings of
credibility and of fact that were crucial to the determination of
the issues between the parties. In the reasons, serious
discrepancies in the evidence adduced at the trial were not only
unresolved, but were, in large measure, unaddressed. To draw the
inferences that counsel invited us to make on the argument of
this appeal would be no more than guesswork on our part and would
not be fair to the parties.


[3]  Accordingly, we agree, regretfully, with the submission of
counsel for the appellants that a new trial must be held.


[4]  The appeal is allowed, the judgment at trial is set aside,
and a new trial is ordered. The costs of the trial and of this
appeal are reserved for determination by the judge presiding at
the new trial.
