                      ENDORSEMENT
                                

[1]  The appellants appeal from that portion of the judgment of
Clarke J. in which he decided that an impugned portion of a
codicil dated August 8, 1990 was written prior to the signing of
the codicil and was a valid part of that codicil.  The trial
judge carefully considered the evidence that the impugned portion
appeared to have been written in a different ink than other
portions of the codicil, the squeezed style of writing, the fact
that the writing in the impugned portion went into the margin of
the document, the overwriting of the date at the end of the
document and the evidence of the witnesses including that of the
appellant=s expert.


[2]  The trial judge concluded nevertheless that the impugned
portion had been inserted  prior to the time the testator signed
the document and gave reasons for so doing. We cannot say that
the trial judge=s conclusion was unreasonable and accordingly we
would dismiss the appeal.


[3]  The appellants also appeal the trial judge=s award of costs.
In making his award as to costs, the trial judge took into
consideration certain factors that were irrelevant to the
question of costs such as the fact that the respondent had given
gifts to the testator during her lifetime and her financial
situation. However, he also considered the mixed success at trial
and in our view, removing the improper considerations, the award
as to costs was reasonable in the circumstances.


[4]  The appeal is dismissed. Costs of the appeal are to the
respondent.
