                          ENDORSEMENT

[1]  There is no proper basis on which we can interfere with the
convictions. We are not persuaded that the convictions were
unreasonable.  We can appreciate how the jury could have had a
reasonable doubt on the physical assault counts and also been
satisfied beyond a reasonable doubt of the guilt of the appellant
on the sexual assault counts.


[2]  There was no error in failing to give a caution respecting
the evidence of the complainant. The trial judge charged the jury
on the care they were required to take respecting credibility. It
cannot be said that she erred in the exercise of her discretion
on this question in light of neither defence counsel requesting a
direction of this kind.


[3]  There was no prejudice respecting the instruction relating
to the Albanian criminal conviction, the evidence of which had
been adduced by the defence. The trial judge instructed the jury
that the criminal record would be of very little assistance in
judging the appellant's credibility. There was no objection to
the charge in this regard.


[4]  When the trial judge's charge bearing on the point is
examined in its context, we do not think that there was any error
respecting improper use of a prior consistent statement. There
was no objection to the charge on this issue either.


[5]  The conviction on the charge for attempted anal intercourse
is set aside. We accede to counsels' submissions that the facts
relating to it should be considered in relation to the conviction
for sexual assault.


[6]  Respecting the sentence, we are not persuaded that there is
any error in principle in the trial judge's reasons or that the
sentence imposed is manifestly unreasonable. Having regard to the
appellant's age and health, we recommend that the sentence be
served in the Guelph Correctional Institution.


[7]  Subject to setting aside the conviction on the count for
attempted anal intercourse, the appeal from convictions is set aside.

Leave to appeal sentence is granted but the appeal is dismissed.
