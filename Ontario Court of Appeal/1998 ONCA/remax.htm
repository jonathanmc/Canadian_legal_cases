                          ENDORSEMENT
[1]  In our view, the trial judge erred in concluding that the
appellant Apollo was not a registered real estate broker on the
basis that, although in fact it was registered under the Act, it
should not, in her view, have been registered. According to the
ordinary meaning of the term, Apollo was registered and it was
not open to the trial judge to engraft onto this ordinary meaning
the requirement referred to by her.




[2]  Further, if resort is properly to be had to the Act, it
appears from ss. 8(2) and 9 that there is no automatic revocation
of registration under the Act if a registrant is in breach of a
term or condition of the registration. This was not a decision
that was open to the trial judge to make.

[3]  With respect to the requirement in the listing agreement

relating to "the amount actually paid", with which the trial

judge did not deal, we are not persuaded that the payment which

clearly was made did not meet the requirement.

[4]  The appeal is allowed, with costs, the judgment is set

aside, and the action is dismissed with costs.
