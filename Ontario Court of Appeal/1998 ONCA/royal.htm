                          ENDORSEMENT

[1]  Having regard to the careful circumscription of the evidence
in Mr. DiVito's affidavit that he had "no recollection of being
requested to sign or of signing a personal guarantee", to the
undisputed fact that he signed a number of documents relating to
the small business loan given by the respondent, and to the
correspondence between the guarantee itself and terms appearing
in those related documents, we are of the view that it was open
to MacPherson J. to conclude, as he did, that Mr. DiVito signed
the guarantee on which he was held liable to the respondent.


[2]  The suggested issue of mitigation, with which MacPherson J.
did not deal in his endorsement, is completely answered in the
affidavit filed by the respondent on the motion for summary
judgment. The appellant put forward no evidence which would call
into question the force of the respondent's evidence on that
subject.


[3]  Accordingly, the appeal is dismissed with costs, fixed in
the amount of $1,500.
