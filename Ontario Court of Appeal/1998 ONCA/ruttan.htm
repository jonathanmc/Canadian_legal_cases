                          ENDORSEMENT


The issue described as "similar fact evidence"


[1]  The appellant was content to proceed to trial on this five-
count indictment and made no application to sever the two "arson"
counts. The trial judge did instruct the jury:

          ... In arriving at your verdicts you are to
          consider each count on its own and to arrive
          at your verdict on it without consideration
          to the evidence adduced on the other counts.

[2]  The passage in the next part of the charge, on which the
appellant particularly relies, did not contradict this but would
be understood as being subject to it. There was no objection to
the charge on this issue or request that the trial judge go
further with respect to special instructions relating to the use
of the evidence on counts 1 and 4.


Missing Transcript


[3]  We are satisfied that this has occasioned no injustice to
the appellant. The defence based on the anticipated evidence of
Mr. Rosen was substantially put to the jury.


Expert Evidence


[4]  Having regard to the role of the expert evidence in this
case on each side, the charge contained no error and was
adequate.


Consciousness of Guilt


[5]  The charge on this issue, apart from being unduly favourable
to the defence with respect to what was said relating to the
standard of proof, was accurate and in its context would have
been understood by the jury as having application only to count 1.


Admission of Guilt


[6]  Although the trial judge used descriptive words that were
not appropriate, we have no doubt that the appellant's responses
to Mrs. Quan's and Detective LeFave's statements were
incriminating and that there was no material error in the
instruction to the jury on this subject.


Other Submissions


[7]  There was no error in the conduct of the trial respecting
the exclusion of the evidence of Jason Bell, and matters related
to it, and Mr. Hicks rightly did not press the argument on this
ground, nor on the ground relating to the admissibility of the
appellant's statements to Detective LeFave and Mr. LaBrash.


Sentence


[8]  Although the sentence imposed may be at the high end of the
range, the reasons for imposing it do not reflect any error in
principle, nor is the sentence itself demonstrably unfit.


Disposition


[9]  For these reasons the appeal from conviction is dismissed.
Leave to appeal sentence is granted, but the appeal is dismissed.
