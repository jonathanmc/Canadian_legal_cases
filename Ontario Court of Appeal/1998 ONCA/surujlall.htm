                          ENDORSEMENT

[1]  We are satisfied that the evidence before the trial judge
was reasonably capable of supporting his finding that the
appellant was the person who stabbed Mr. Dennettt. Mr. Dennett
had identified the appellant three times very shortly before the
stabbing, two of these times with a knife in his hand. The
appellant himself confirmed the correctness of two of these
sightings, although he did not admit that he had a knife in his
hand at any time. If Mr. Dennett's evidence did not amount to
direct evidence that it was the appellant who stabbed him, it was
very strong circumstantial evidence to this effect. We are not
persuaded that the trial judge's reasons contain any material
error.


[2]  Respecting sentence, this was a very serious, mindless act
of violence that came within millimetres of killing Mr. Dennett.
There is no error in principle disclosed in the trial judge's
reasons, nor is the sentence manifestly unreasonable.


[3]  The appeal from conviction is dismissed. Leave to appeal
sentence is granted but the appeal is dismissed.
