                     ENDORSEMENT

[1]    In our view the motions judge misapprehended the evidence
on the issue of the relationship between the parties Tate and
CTI.  All of the evidence supports the appellant's position that
Tate and CTI were in a purchaser-vendor relationship common in
commercial transactions of this sort and not an agency or special
agency relationship. There is no evidence from the actions and
statements of the parties at the time of the transaction that the
monies forwarded by Tate were to be held in trust by CTI.  The
funds were put into CTI's general bank account by CTI.  Its bank
took approximately $1000 to pay down an overdraft.  Cheques were
outstanding drawn by CTI on that account.  The fact that
representatives of  Tate and CTI may have stated in this
proceeding after the fact that the monies belong to Tate does not
assist in the legal characterization when the actions of those
parties do not bear out that characterization.


[2]    As there was no agency and no trust the funds were
properly seized by the sheriff and are to be distributed by the
sheriff in accordance with the sheriff's obligations in law.


[3]    The appeal is therefore allowed, the order below set aside
and the motion dismissed with costs to the appellant.
