                      ENDORSEMENT
                                
                                
[1]  Stan Whitty moves for an order extending the time for the
filing of a notice of appeal from the order of Cameron J. of the
Ontario Court (General Division) dated July 2, 1998. Cameron J.
found the moving party in contempt of the judgment of Dunnet J.
as modified by the order of Chapnik J. He also seeks a stay of
the contempt order of Cameron J. directing that he sell his Motor
Home vehicle within 60 days and extending the order of Dunnet J.
that he refrain from parking the vehicle within a defined area of
his residence.


[2]  There is a long history of friction between the moving party
and first, the City of  Etobicoke, and now the City of Toronto
with respect to his attempts to park his Motor Home in front of
or in the driveway of his residence. He has failed at every level
to either acquire the necessary permission from the municipality
or to persuade the appropriate court that he should be permitted
to park his oversized vehicle on or near his residence in
contravention of the zoning by-law.


[3]  It appears to me that he has received a great deal of
leniency from the court and I can think of no reason to extend
him a further indulgence. While he protests that he was ready and
willing to initiate an appeal from the contempt order of Cameron
J. within the time limited for appeal, a careful reading of the
record indicates to me that he was only using the threat of an
appeal to negotiate concessions from the municipality with
respect to being allowed to continue parking the vehicle. He
brought this motion after rejecting the final offer from the
responding party and in the face of a further application to the
court for directions as to how to proceed with the sale of the
Motor Home under the contempt order.


[4]  Additionally, it is apparent that he was aware that his
appeal lacked merit. This appears from the last letter that his
former solicitor sent to the solicitor acting for the responding
party. He stated on September 2, 1998:

                    I write to advise you that I have
          indicated to Mr Whitty that I am unable to
          conclude that the decision of Mr. Justice
          Cameron should be appealed. I understand that
          Mr. Whitty may retain other counsel to advise
          him and perhaps to appeal.
          
[5]       The one matter that concerned me was whether Cameron J.
had the right to order the moving party to sell the Motor Home
that was the instrument of his contempt. On reflection, I think
that in the unique circumstances of the contempt in this case, he
was entitled to rely upon Rule 60.11(5)(f) of the Rules of Civil
Procedure, which authorises the judge making the finding of
contempt to order the contemner to "comply with any order that
the judge considers necessary". Since the moving party had
obstinately and consistently refused to abide by the restrictions
imposed on parking such a vehicle and because it was the
vehicle's presence on or near his residence that constituted the
act of contempt, Cameron J. was entitled to compel him to dispose
of the offending vehicle.


[6]  For the reasons set out above, I am of the opinion that this
motion is not made in good faith. It is yet another step in the
ongoing conflict between the parties and reflects a stubborn
refusal of the moving party to accept that he must comply with
the zoning provisions in his municipality. There is no reason why
this conflict should be further protracted.


[7]  Accordingly, the motion is dismissed with costs.
