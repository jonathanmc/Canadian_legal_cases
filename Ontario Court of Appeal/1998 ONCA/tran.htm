                      ENDORSEMENT
                                
[1]  The only issue in this personal injury action was whether
the plaintiff=s injuries met the threshold requirement for
exemption to the schedule of no-fault benefits under s. 266 of
the Insurance Act, R.S.O. 1990, c. I. 8.


[2]  The parties at trial agreed that the first two of the
questions approved by this court in Meyer v. Bright (1993), 15
O.R. (3d) 129 (C.A.) were to be answered in the affirmative. That
is to say that the plaintiff has sustained permanent impairment
of a bodily function caused by a continuing injury which is
physical in nature and that the bodily function is an important
one. The disagreement was limited to whether the impairment to
the bodily function was serious.


[3]  Meyer (at p. 141) made it clear that each of these cases
must be decided upon its own facts. We are not in a position to
say that the trial judge was wrong in finding that the impairment
was serious to this particular plaintiff, the respondent in this
appeal.


[4]  Accordingly, the appeal is dismissed with costs.
