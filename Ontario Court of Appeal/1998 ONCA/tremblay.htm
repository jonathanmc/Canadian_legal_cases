                     ENDORSEMENT
[1]    On August 1, 1997, E. Loukidelis J. gave judgment in this
matter.  A notice of appeal was served and filed on behalf of a
creditor, Rose Anne Tremblay, by her then solicitor.


[2]    The appellant made inquiries of her solicitor from time to
time as to the progress of the appeal as is made clear by the
correspondence filed on this motion.  There was no response from
her solicitor or his office.


[3]    As a result, the appellant gave notice by a letter dated
August 20, 1998 that she would act on her own behalf.  Before
that notice was received by the Court of Appeal office, the
Registrar sent notice to her former solicitor of the Registrar's
intention to dismiss her appeal for delay.  This was sent on to
the appellant by her solicitor's office.  The appellant then
prepared and forwarded the materials required to perfect the
appeal.


[4]    The appellant's response, however, was not in sufficient
time to prevent the Registrar from making the order dismissing
the appeal for delay.  This was on October 2, 1998.


[5]    When it was pointed out to the appellant that in order to
proceed she would have to get that order set aside, this motion
was launched.


[6]    It is obvious from the material filed that the appellant
has always intended to appeal and to prosecute her appeal.
Accordingly, the order of the Registrar of October 2, 1998
dismissing the appeal for delay is set aside.  The time for
perfecting the appeal is extended to November 30, 1998.


[7]    This matter was heard by teleconference on November 5,
1998.  The bankrupt, Ronald Arthur Tremblay, did not participate
although duly served with notice of this matter.  The telephone
operator advised those participating in the call that she had
attempted to reach him but that all she was able to get was a
recorded message.
