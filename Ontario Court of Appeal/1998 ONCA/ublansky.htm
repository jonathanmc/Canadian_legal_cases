                     ENDORSEMENT
[1]    The motions judge was concerned with the genuineness of
the issue raised for trial, that is, whether the 1989 mortgage
was forged as alleged in the proposed statement of defence.


[2]    The defendants swore in their affidavits that they did not
execute the 1989 mortgage on which they were sued.  However, they
acknowledge that the monies were advanced in 1987, that a further
$100,000 was advanced in 1989, that they paid monthly payments up
to 1996 and that the balance of the monies remain outstanding.


[3]    Their lawyer in 1989, Mr. Carter, swore an affidavit in
which he said that he witnessed their signatures on the 1989
mortgage.  He declined to be cross-examined on the day set for
his examination because he was alerted that he might be sued.
However, the defendants did not later pursue any cross-
examination prior to the motion for summary judgment.


[4]    The plaintiff retained a handwriting expert, Ms. Kruger,
who positively identified the signature of Carlo Barranca on the
1989 mortgage and who tentatively identified the signatures of
Joseph Barranca and Salvatore Romeo.  The expert asked for
further handwriting samples from these two defendants but they
refused.


[5]    The motions judge incorrectly stated in his reasons that
the expert had concluded that both Carlo Barranca and Salvatore
Romeo signed the mortgage.  This was an error in respect of
Salvatore Romeo.  However, it is clear that had the motions judge
properly found that the expert had only a tentative opinion about
Salvatore Romeo's signature, he would have drawn an adverse
inference against him from the refusal to provide further
handwriting samples, as he did with Joseph Barranca.


[6]    We see no error in the conclusion of the motions judge
that the issue raised for trial, that is, the forgery issue, was
not a genuine issue on the evidence, and that it was incumbent on
the defendants in the circumstances to bring forward further
evidence to satisfy the court that the forgery issue raised was a
genuine one.


[7]    The appeal is therefore dismissed with costs.
