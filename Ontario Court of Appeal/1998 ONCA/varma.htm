                     ENDORSEMENT

[1]     Mr.  Varma  has appealed seven orders  striking  out  his
statement  of  claim  in seven actions on the  grounds  that  the
statements of claim do not disclose a reasonable cause of action,
are frivolous, vexatious or an abuse of process.


[2]     However,  when  Mr. Varma commenced his  submissions,  it
became apparent that he considered that he was appealing an order
of  Festeryga J., granted February 16, 1998, pursuant to s.140 of
the  Courts  of Justice Act, R.S.O. 1990, c.C.43, which  declared
him to be a vexatious litigant, and inter alia, required that  he
obtain  the leave of a judge of the General Division, as provided
by   s.140(1)(d),  to  continue  any  proceeding  which  he   had
previously  instituted  in the Court of Appeal  and  the  General
Division.  The order was obtained by the respondents to the seven
appeals, as well as by eight other defendants sued by Mr.  Varma.
As  each  of the seven appeals had been commenced prior  to  this
order,  until  the order is reversed, or stayed, Mr.  Varma  must
obtain leave before he can continue them.


[3]     It would appear that Mr. Varma had intended to appeal the
order of Festeryga J. to this court.  However, he did not deliver
his  notice  of appeal within the time required by the  Rules  of
Civil  Procedure.  His application for an extension  of  time  to
serve  his notice of appeal was dismissed by Brooke J.A.  on  May
19, 1998.


[4]     Mr.  Varma attempted to file an application for leave  to
appeal  to  the Supreme Court of Canada from the order of  Brooke
J.A.,  but was advised by officials of that court that  he  could
not  do  so,  and  that his recourse was to move to  have  Brooke
J.A.'s  order  set  aside or varied by a  panel  of  this  court:
Courts  of  Justice Act, s.7(5). This advice  was  correct.   Mr.
Varma  urged us to treat the proceedings as a motion to set aside
Brooke J.A.'s order.  However, as a notice of motion had not been
filed,  and  as  the respondents did not have the opportunity  to
file  an appeal book and a factum, we did not feel that we  could
do so.


[5]    The order of Festeryga J. stands until such time as it  is
reversed  or stayed.  Accordingly, it is our view that his  order
precludes Mr. Varma from proceeding with the seven appeals before
this  court  unless  he obtains leave to  do  so.   We  are  also
satisfied that Festeryga J. had the power under s.140(1) to  make
an  order  affecting proceedings before the Court of Appeal.   In
our  opinion,  "proceeding  ... in any court"  in  s.140(1)(c)(d)
includes  a  proceeding in the Court of Appeal, and  "proceeding"
includes an appeal.  See Mascan Corp. v. French (1988),  64  O.R.
(2d) 1 at 6-7 (C.A.).


[6]     Section  140(4)  governs  an  application  for  leave  to
continue  a  proceeding affected by an order under s.140(1).   We
note  that s.140(4)(b)(c) empowers the judge from whom  leave  to
continue  a  proceeding  is requested to  rescind  the  order  of
Festeryga  J. Thus, Mr. Varma has two paths available to  him  to
attack this order.


[7]    In the circumstances, for the above reasons, Mr. Varma  is
unable to proceed with the seven appeals at this time.


[8]     It  is ordered that the appeals are stayed pending  leave
being  obtained to continue any, or all, of them, or  until  such
time  as  the  order  of  Festeryga J. is  set  aside,  reversed,
rescinded or  stayed.
