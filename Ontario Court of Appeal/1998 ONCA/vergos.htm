                      ENDORSEMENT
                                

[1]  In order to succeed in his claim under the policy against
the defendant insurer, the plaintiff bore the onus of
establishing the actual cash value of the stolen equipment. In
the particular circumstances of this case, there was no other
head of damages for which the insurer could be held responsible.


[2]  In his reasons for judgment, the trial judge accurately
summarized the evidence which the plaintiff relied on in support
of his contention that the stolen equipment had a cash value of
$75,000 as of the date of the theft. Taking into account the
nature and source of this evidence, the trial judge found himself
unable to place a cash value on the stolen equipment.
Accordingly, he concluded that the plaintiff had failed to prove
the cash value of the missing equipment.


[3]  In our view, it was open to the trial judge to make that
finding and we see no basis for interfering with it. In
particular, we reject the submission of the plaintiff, advanced
on the cross-appeal, that the trial judge misapprehended the
evidence as it related to this issue and that his finding was
unreasonable.


[4]  Once it was determined that the actual cash value had not
been established, that should have ended the matter. The
plaintiff had failed to prove his claim and the action should
have been dismissed.


[5]  The trial judge, however, went on to award damages to the
plaintiff for the lease payments he had made in respect of the
stolen insured equipment. On appeal, the parties agreed that
there was no basis in law for such an award and neither sought to
uphold it. We agree.


[6]  The appeal is allowed, the judgment below is set aside and
its place an order will go dismissing the action. The cross-
appeal fails.


[7]  The defendant is entitled to its costs at trial and on
appeal. The cross-appeal is dismissed without costs.
