                     ENDORSEMENT

[1]  The Crown applies for leave to appeal the judgment of the
summary conviction appeal court dismissing a Crown appeal from
the acquittal of the respondent in provincial court on the charge
of "blowing over." The summary conviction appeal court judgment
was released in May, 1995. The Crown perfected its appeal in
June, 1998, some 3 years after the judgment appealed from was
released. Under the rules, the Crown is obliged to perfect its
appeal within 60 days: see Rule 18(4)(a) Criminal Appeal Rules.
The Crown offers no explanation for its failure to perfect in a
timely fashion and its failure to abide by the Rules.


[2]  In our view, if the Crown chooses to launch an appeal and
prolong an accused's jeopardy, particularly where the accused has
successfully defended the charge at trial and at the first level
of appeal, the Crown must move promptly to perfect the appeal.
The Crown's failure to prosecute this appeal with anything
approximating reasonable diligence and its total disregard for
the applicable criminal appeals rules warrants refusal of leave
to appeal.


[3]  Leave to appeal is refused and the application is dismissed.
Counsel may make written submissions as to costs.
