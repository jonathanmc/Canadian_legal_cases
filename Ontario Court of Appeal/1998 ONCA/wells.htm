                     ENDORSEMENT
[1]    Ms. Wells was convicted on May 12, 1997 on charges of
dangerous driving and unlawfully causing bodily harm.  She was
sentenced on June 23, 1997 to 90 days incarceration concurrent on
each conviction and prohibited from driving for 22 months.  Her
summary conviction appeal with respect to convictions and
sentences was dismissed.


[2]    She now seeks leave to appeal both convictions and
sentences, a stay on the driving prohibition and judicial interim
release pending the hearing of the appeal.


[3]    As to convictions, it is alleged that the trial judge
misapprehended the evidence in two respects and that the summary
conviction appeal judge failed to correct those misapprehensions
and thereby erred in law.  A review of the evidence in question
makes it clear that the conclusions drawn by the trial judge were
supported by that evidence.  The summary conviction appeal judge
accordingly did not err in "failing to correct" those findings.


[4]    It is not at all clear that the trial judge erred in
refusing to apply the principle in Kienapple.  In any event, no
prejudice was suffered by Ms. Wells as the equal sentences are
concurrent.


[5]    As to sentence, the remarks of the trial judge respecting
conditional sentences and general deterrence do not constitute
any error in law.


[6]    There is, accordingly, no basis upon which leave should be
granted to appeal either convictions or sentences.  The
application for leave to appeal the convictions and sentences is
thereby dismissed as is her application to stay the driving
prohibition and her application for judicial interim release.
