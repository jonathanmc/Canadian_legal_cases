                      ENDORSEMENT
                                

[1]  The appellants submit that the questions and answers (294 to
298)  during the discovery of Mr. Catalano constituted  a  formal
offer  and acceptance of a settlement and that the motions  judge
erred  in  not  finding  that there is  such  a  settlement.   We
disagree.
[2]  The appellant’s position is belied by its own conduct
immediately after the above noted exchange.  First, Mr. Strung
did not tender the $10,000. He did not do what Mr. Catalano said
needed to be done in his final answer “ give it to me now and I
will leave”.  Second, Mr. Strung continued with the discovery.
In fact, he asked Mr. Catalano sixty-five more questions.  This
is inconsistent with the existence of a settlement.  Indeed, the
inference is that Mr. Strung himself did not think there was a
settlement at that junction.
[3]  The appeal is dismissed with costs.
