                      ENDORSEMENT
                                

[1]   Although  the  information contained the signature  of  the
informant, the signature was illegible. In our view, the  absence
of a typed or legible signature of the informant did not make the
information  so  fundamentally defective that it  amounted  to  a
nullity. We find the reasoning of the Nova Scotia Court of Appeal
in   Wells  (1979),  52  A.P.R.  395  (N.S.S.C.A.D.)  persuasive.
Therefore,  we  agree  with the summary conviction  appeal  court
judge  that the information did not deprive the provincial  court
judge of jurisdiction.
[2]  The appeal is therefore dismissed.
