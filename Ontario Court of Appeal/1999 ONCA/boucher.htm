On appeal from the judgment of Ferrier J. dated April 20, 1998.
                      ENDORSEMENT
                                

[1]   In  our view this appeal turns on the findings of  fact  at
trial.
[2]  The critical finding of fact was that the contract agreed to
by the parties required that the appellant pay the $40,000 in
order to get the shares. In making this finding the trial judge
accepted Mr. Kennedy’s evidence that this was “the exact deal”.
Given that the finding is supported by this evidence we cannot
interfere, since this precondition was not met. The trial judge
properly concluded that the appellant never became a shareholder.
[3]  On the evidence there is no other basis for an oppression
remedy.
[4]  As to the contempt finding, the breach of the order of
Montgomery J. was admitted. The assessment of penalty is, in our
view, a matter for the discretion of the trial judge. We see no
error in principle in the penalty imposed and we would not
interfere.
[5]  Therefore, in spite of Mr. Leitch’s excellent presentation,
the appeal must be dismissed with costs.
