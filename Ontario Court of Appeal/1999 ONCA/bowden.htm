                      ENDORSEMENT


[1]  With respect to the trial judge, we are of the view that he
committed several errors in principle in sentencing the appellant
to a fine of $3,000.


[2]  First, the trial judge placed undue emphasis on the
uniformity principle and in doing so, he failed to pay adequate
attention to the individual offender and the particular facts and
circumstances giving rise to his offence.


[3]  In this case, the error was significant because it
effectively removed from the trial judge's consideration the low
level of moral culpability attributable to the appellant. In this
regard, there was ample evidence capable of supporting the
appellant’s position that he honestly, albeit mistakenly, thought
he was not breaking the law. This belief on his part was borne
out by the fact that he was declaring the gaming revenue as
income for tax purposes, as well as the fact that the illegal
gaming was not brought to his attention by the many police
officers and court officials who attended his restaurant on a
regular basis.


[4]  Second, the trial judge placed undue emphasis on the fact
that this was a large scale operation being carried out by many
people in the area. While that may be true, there is no
suggestion that the appellant was aware of the extent of the
problem or that he participated in any way in the illegal
activity carried on by others.


[5]  In view of these errors, we need not show deference to the
sentence imposed by the trial judge.


[6]  Bearing in mind the appellant’s prior unblemished background
and history, his plea of guilty and remorse, the small amount of
profit he derived and his degree of moral culpability, we believe
that this was an appropriate case for a discharge.


[7]  At the time of the sentence hearing, we think a conditional
discharge with probation, including among other terms, a
community service order, would have been a fit and just sentence.
Given the passage of time and the fact that the appellant had
been trouble free in the interim (a period of four years), we are
of the view that an absolute discharge is called for.


[8]  In the result, leave to appeal sentence is granted, the
appeal is allowed and an absolute discharge is imposed.
