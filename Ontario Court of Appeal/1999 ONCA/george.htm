                      ENDORSEMENT
                                

[1]  The Crown does not now rely on the allegation that the
statement was false as circumstantial evidence of guilt.  This
leaves the fact that on a hot July night the appellant was
perspiring and that he and his car were in the area thirty
minutes after the robbery.  That would not amount to a basis upon
which a preliminary inquiry judge could form an opinion that the
evidence was sufficient.  It amounts to mere suspicion.
[2]  Accordingly, the appeal is allowed and the order to stand
trial is quashed.
