                      ENDORSEMENT
                                

[1]  The disposition judge was quite properly concerned about the
serious nature of this offence which involved an assault upon a
complete stranger at 2:00 o’clock in the morning.  The appellant
used a weapon, an asp.  The victim, who was unarmed, received a
2 inch cut to his head requiring 10 stitches.
[2]  The judge emphasized the need for specific deterrence and
considered that it could be best achieved by a short term of open
custody.  We see no error in principle nor do we think it can be
said that the disposition is manifestly unfit or outside the
appropriate range:  see R. v. Priest (1996), 30 O.R. (3d) 538
(C.A.).
[3]  We cannot leave this appeal without commenting on the
argument that if the custodial term is upheld, the appellant’s
school term and hockey playing schedule will be disrupted.  We
point out that the disposition judge had these concerns in mind
when she delayed the disposition until the appellant’s summer
holiday.   This appeal is what has created the problem that is
now raised.
[4]  The appeal is dismissed.
