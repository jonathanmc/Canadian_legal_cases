                      ENDORSEMENT


[1]  The appellant raised several grounds of appeal against his
conviction. He also seeks leave to appeal and if leave is granted
to appeal his sentence.


The Section 8 Violation


[2]  We see no error in the trial judge’s reasons or with his
conclusion on this issue. We also accept as correct the trial
judge’s reasons and his conclusion with respect to the validity
of the search warrant.


The Actus Reus Issue


[3]  The complainants paid $100,000 to the appellant “in trust”.
The funds were to be deposited in a separate bank account. The
appellant manifestly used the trust funds for his own purposes.
Although the complainants had parted with their money, they
continued, as beneficiaries of the trust, to have a property
interest in it. In the particular circumstances, we do not accept
the submission that upon delivery of the trust funds to the
appellant, the funds belonged to the appellant. We think,
therefore, that the actus reus of the offence charged (theft by
conversion) was made out. Moreover, the verdict was not
unreasonable.


The Mens Rea Issue


[4]  The appellant submits that the trial judge failed to
adequately direct the jury on the mens rea of theft by
conversion. In particular, the appellant contends that the trial
judge should have explicitly directed the jury on the issue of
“colour of right.”


[5]  The trial judge made it clear to the jury that in order to
convict the appellant, they had to be satisfied beyond a
reasonable doubt that the appellant and the complainants “... had
a clear understanding on both sides that the money would be used
only for the franchise agreement.”  The trial judge also stated
repeatedly that to convict the appellant, the jury had to be
satisfied beyond a reasonable doubt that he intended to act
dishonestly in the manner in which he used the complainants’
money.


[6]  No objection was taken to that part of the charge dealing
with mens rea, and in our view, the trial judge correctly
directed the jury on that issue. He had no obligation to
specifically use the words “colour of right.”


Sentence


[7]  This was a serious breach of trust which involved the
retirement savings of one of the victims. The sentence imposed by
the trial judge was, in our view, fit. The conditional sentence
provisions of the Criminal Code were not available to the trial
judge when he sentenced the appellant. However, we do not think
that a conditional sentence is warranted in this case.


[8]  A compensation order was clearly called for. However, the
appellant takes issue with the quantum of the compensation order.
In our opinion, when he dealt with the compensation order, the
trial judge applied the correct principles and although the
appellant applied a small part of the trust funds to the
franchise, his actions, mainly within 48 hours of his receipt of
the trust funds, made it impossible for him to comply with the
provisions of the trust. Accordingly, we think the $100,000
compensation order was justified in the circumstances and we are
not prepared to interfere with it.


[9]  The community service order was an integral of the sentence
imposed by the trial judge. We are not persuaded that there is a
basis upon which we should reduce it.


CONCLUSION


[10] Accordingly, the leave against conviction is dismissed.
Leave to appeal sentence is granted and the appeal against
sentence is dismissed.
