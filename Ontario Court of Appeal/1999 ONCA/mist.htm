                      ENDORSEMENT
                                

[1]  This is a motion to quash the appeal on the grounds that the
only  stated  basis  for the appeal is that Farley  J.  erred  in
refusing  the  plaintiff’s request for an  adjournment  when  the
matter  came before him on the Commercial List.  This sole ground
of  appeal,  contends the moving party, is  with  respect  to  an
interlocutory matter; accordingly, the necessary appeal route  is
to the Divisional Court with leave, not to this court.
[2]  In our view, the motion should be dismissed.  There are
three paragraphs in Farley J.’s order.  The second paragraph
dismisses the plaintiff’s entire action.
[3]  The appellant has appealed Farley J.’s entire order
including the second paragraph. The specific relief sought on the
appeal, namely an order that Farley J. erred in refusing to grant
an adjournment, would lead automatically to a new hearing.  In
short, the whole matter would be reopened.  Paragraph 2 of Farley
J.’s order, which the moving party concedes is a final order,
would fall with the entire order if the appeal is allowed.
Accordingly, Farley J.’s order is, in our view, a final order.
[4]  We direct that the appeal be expedited.
[5]  The costs of this motion are in the cause on the appeal.
