                      ENDORSEMENT
                                
[1]  These appeals (C29309 and C29310) must be dismissed.


[2]  The plaintiffs commenced separate actions in 1993 for
alleged medical malpractice.  The actions were conducted by a
solicitor until April 1996 and have been conducted by the
plaintiffs personally since that time.


[3]  They have, however, repeatedly refused to produce medical
information and to sign releases or directions to facilitate
discovery.  This, despite orders to do so.  Instead, they have
consistently blamed others for the fact that the action has
stalled:  first their lawyer, then the defendant’s lawyers and
now the courts.


[4]  Although they have been granted extensions for some years,
they have failed to comply with the Rules or orders
notwithstanding the imposition of ultimata since July 5, 1996.


[5]  The appellants are correct when they say that the defendant
moved quickly when the reasons of Boland J. were released,
refusing leave to appeal from the order of Cumming J. of December
22, 1997.  They cannot complain in that regard nor suggest that
they were taken by surprise.  They were told in unmistakable
terms that when those reasons were released, if leave was
refused, the defendant would move immediately.  In any event,
they had ample opportunity to comply with the requirement
specified by Cumming J. long before those reasons were released.


[6]  There is nothing to be gained by repeating what has already
been said, particularly by Cumming J. in his reasons of December
22, 1997.  Each of his findings is soundly based upon the
evidence before the court.  Even today, there is no indication on
the part of the appellants that they have any intention of
complying with the requirements of the Rules.  Instead, they wish
to set aside the orders of Cumming J., Boland J. and Master
Garfield, the latter having been made some years ago.


[7]  These appeals are therefore dismissed, with costs, if asked.
