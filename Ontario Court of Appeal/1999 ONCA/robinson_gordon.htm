                      ENDORSEMENT

[1]  At the opening of this appeal, counsel for the respondent
raised the issue of the court’s jurisdiction. After reviewing the
matter and counsel’s submissions with respect to that issue, we
were satisfied that we did have jurisdiction. We thus heard
argument on the appeal.

[2]  The appellant raised three grounds of appeal.
     
     1.        Unreasonable Verdict
[3]  In our view, there was ample evidence to support all of the
trial judge’s findings including his assessment of damages. The
record does not reveal palpable or overriding error. Further, we
think that the trial judge’s reasons are sufficient. They reveal
that he was alert to the central trial issues and to the
positions taken by the parties.
     
     2.        The Unwarranted Interference by the Trial Judge
[4]  We have reviewed all of the trial judge’s interventions as
set out in the appellant’s factum and Ms. Balaban’s oral argument
with respect to this issue. We see absolutely no merit in this
ground of appeal.
     
     3.        Costs
[5]  We think that in the circumstances, particularly having
regard to the plaintiff’s settlement offer, it was open to the
trial judge to order the appellant to pay solicitor and client
costs from the date on which the trial commenced.

[6]  For these reasons, the appeal is dismissed with costs.
