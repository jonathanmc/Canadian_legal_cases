                      ENDORSEMENT

[1]  We do not think that given the narrow scope of certiorari,
the motions judge erred in dismissing the application.  There was
nothing in the circumstances of this case to require a Provincial
Court judge to conduct an inquiry concerning the appellant’s
waiver, through counsel, of the preliminary inquiry.  Thus, the
committing judge, in our view, committed no jurisdictional error.
[2]  The issue of the ineffective assistance of counsel raised in
the appellant’s affidavit can be dealt with on appeal if the
appellant is convicted, or perhaps by way of an application
seeking Charter relief at trial.
[3]  Thus, the appeal is dismissed.
