On appeal from the summary conviction appeal judgment given by

                     E N D O R S E M E N T 
[1]  This is a Crown appeal on a question of law alone from the
decision of Valin J., sitting as a summary conviction appeal
court judge.  The respondent had been convicted by Fournier J. of
sexual assault arising out of the touching of the eight-year-old
complainant when he was acting as her babysitter.
[2]  The question of law the Crown puts forward in this court is
that Valin J. erred in law in his analysis of the trial judge’s
self-instruction on the burden of proof.  This is an analysis
that is restricted to the particular facts of this case and the
specific language used by the trial judge.
[3]  In the circumstances of this case, we are not prepared to
grant leave to appeal.  Application dismissed.
