A D D E N D U M

BY THE COURT:

[1]               The court has received and reviewed the written submissions of the appellants and the respondent concerning the costs of the appeal.

[2]               The trial judge, after hearing substantial argument, issued reasons awarding costs of the trial only against the Litigation Guardian. She declined to award those costs against the injured plaintiff or the Family Law Act plaintiff. The respondent did not appeal from this disposition.

[3]               We agree with the reasoning of the trial judge. In our view, the costs order on appeal should parallel that made at trial. Given the grave injuries suffered by the individual plaintiff, it was the Litigation Guardian who commenced the proceedings and undertook the costs risks attendant thereon. The Family Law Act claim took little, if any, of the time of this proceeding. Hence neither Hugh John McMaster nor Anne Catherine MacMaster should bear the respondent’s costs on appeal.

[4]               In the circumstances, costs of the appeal should be payable only by the Litigation Guardian.
