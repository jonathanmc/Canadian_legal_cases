On appeal from the order of Sharpe J. dated September 1, 1998.

                      ENDORSEMENT
                                

[1]  We see no basis for interfering with the order of Sharpe J.
[2]  His determination that in all the circumstances Ms.
Millington had not moved promptly enough to set aside the default
judgment is, in our view, reasonable.
[3]  Equally, his finding of fact that Mr. Phillips received
notice both of the sale and the proceedings before Mr. Justice
Cullity is supportable on this record.
[4]  Finally, we agree with his interpretation of the
Conveyancing and Law of Property Act. The judgment creditor is,
in these circumstances, not the seller.
[5]  The appeal must therefore be dismissed with costs.
