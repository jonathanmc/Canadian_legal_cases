APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1] We have not been asked to reconsider our prior authorities on this question of law. Counsel has attempted to distinguish those authorities from this case.

[2] We agree with Durno J.'s analysis of those authorities. As we read those authorities, it is not necessary for the officer making the "alert" demand to know or believe the person to whom the demand is made was operating, or was in care and control of the vehicle: see R. v. MacPherson, [2000] O.J. 4777, paras. 4-6.

[3] We would grant leave to appeal but would dismiss the appeal.
