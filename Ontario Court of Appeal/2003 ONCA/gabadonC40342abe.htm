APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1] We agree with the reasons of the motions judge. In Nelles v. Ontario and in Proulx v. Quebec, the Supreme Court of Canada emphasized that the court cannot merely second-guess the prosecutor; there must be evidence of malice. Not only did the appellant not adduce evidence of malice, but the record demonstrates an absence of malice. The respondent chose not to call an unsavoury witness to prevent a wrongful conviction. In those circumstances, there was no basis for inferring malice from the possible absence of reasonable and probable grounds.

[2] Appeal is dismissed.

[3] Leave to appeal costs is refused.

[4] The respondent is entitled to costs fixed at $2,500.
