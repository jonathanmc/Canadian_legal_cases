APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1] The trial judge gave careful and extensive reasons in which he considered, but rejected on the facts of this case, the propriety of a conditional sentence. He made no error in principle or any error that would warrant appellate intervention. This was a serious offence, involving the threat of a knife against a vulnerable victim.

[2] Leave to appeal against sentence is granted but the appeal against sentence is dismissed.
