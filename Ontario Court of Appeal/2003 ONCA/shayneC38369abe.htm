On appeal from the conviction entered by Justice P. Lalonde of the Superior Court of Justice, sitting with a jury, on December 3, 2001 and from the sentence imposed by Justice P. Lalonde of the Superior Court of Justice on May 16, 2002.

APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1] The main witness against the accused was an accomplice. The accused gave evidence and contradicted the accomplice's evidence. In his charge, the trial judge, at pp. 50-51, stated:

You, as triers of fact, have to decide whom you believe. …You're required to determine if Mr. Kerry W. Shayne's version of the events are true or could reasonably be true.

[2] In our view, notwithstanding that a proper W.D. charge was also given to the jury, this instruction constitutes reversible error. In light of this conclusion, it is unnecessary to deal with the other grounds of appeal.

[3] Accordingly, the appeal is allowed, the conviction is set aside, and a new trial ordered.
