On appeal from the judgment of Justice James B. Chadwick of the Superior Court of Justice, sitting with a jury, dated April 17, 2003.

APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1] The appellant exercised its rights to have the action tried by a jury. No objection was taken at trial by the appellant to the charge to the jury. As the trial judge noted, credibility was a central issue in this case. The jury verdict must be treated with considerable respect and must be accorded great weight. We are not persuaded that there is any basis upon which to interfere with this verdict.

[2] The respondent is entitled to her costs of the appeal fixed in the amount of $20,000, inclusive of disbursements and Goods and Services Tax.
