On appeal from the conviction entered by Justice Gethin B. Edward of the Ontario Court of Justice dated June 23, 2003.

APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1] The appellant submits that the verdict is unreasonable. The appellant admitted his involvement in the robbery to a witness, Mr. Uhryn. Mr. Uhryn had a criminal record and argued that there was bad blood between himself and the appellant. However, the balance of the evidence served to confirm the appellant's admission.

[2] The trial judge was in the best position to assess the reliability and credibility of the witnesses. In addition, the appellant called no evidence to contradict or give an explanation for his admission.

[3] The verdict was not unreasonable. The appeal as to conviction is dismissed.
