APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1] This court has made it clear that it is generally not appropriate to grant summary judgment when the rule of discoverability is central to the resolution of the limitation issue. In our view, the evidence is not clear. There are facts in dispute that constitute a genuine issue for trial.

[2] The trial judge did not deal with the issue of fiduciary duty. This claim is not related to a claim for malpractice in respect of professional services and cannot be subject to limitation in the RHPA.

[3] The appeal is allowed with costs fixed for the motion for summary judgment and the appeal, at $12,000.00 all inclusive.
