On appeal from the decision of Justice Ferguson dated August 10, 1999 and the decision of Justice Stong dated May 22, 2001.

APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]               The appellant appeals from the following two decisions:

The decision of Ferguson J. dated August 10, 1999 in which he dismissed an appeal from the Consent and Capacity Board (the Board) dated May 8, 1998 which found the appellant incapable of consenting to treatment and

The decision of Stong J. dated May 22, 2001 granting the respondents’ motion to quash the appeal from the decision of the Board dated October 27, 1995 declining to appoint Peter Poulakis as the appellant’s representative for the purpose of giving or refusing consent to treatment on the appellant’s behalf.

[2]               The appellant also raised certain constitutional issues before this court which were not raised in any court below and we are therefore not to be able to deal with them.

[3]               With respect to the appeal from Ferguson J., we are of the view that there was evidence before the Board on which it could make a finding of the appellant’s inability to consent to the treatment.  We found no error of law or fact in relation to the finding and the appeal from Ferguson J. is therefore dismissed.

[4]               The appeal from Stong J. is dismissed as the representative had withdrawn his consent, which consent is required pursuant to s. 33(6) of the Health Care Consent Act.

[5]               Finally, in our view, the fact that the appellant was discharged subsequent to the finding of his inability to consent and then readmitted does not engage any relevant issue for this court.
