ENDORSEMENT

[1]               This is a motion to set aside or vary the order of Gillese J.A. who refused a stay pending appeal on the basis that the order appealed was an interlocutory rather than a final order. If the order appealed is interlocutory, this court has no jurisdiction to order a stay. In that case, the matter would fall within the jurisdiction of the Divisional Court, with leave of that court.

[2]               The appeal is taken from the January 2005 order which was based on a December 2004 order. The December 2004 order, which was a consent order, directed the appellants to provide a “full accounting”, an order that the appellants acknowledge before this court was interlocutory. When the parties were unable to agree on the meaning of “full”, the respondents brought a motion before the same motion judge. Although that notice of motion is not before us, it appears that the request was for relief arising from the appellants’ failure to comply with the December order and was brought under Rule 60.12. In considering that motion, the motion judge commented that the appellants’ productions did not “constitute a full accounting”. He then defined what he had earlier meant by a “full” accounting and ordered the appellants to comply with that order.

[3]               The meaning of “full” is not the substantive issue to be resolved at trial. The substantive issue is whether the appellants owe money to the respondents with respect to various mortgages. To determine that issue, the parties consented to a “full” accounting in December. The order for a full accounting is, in substance, an order for relief preparatory for the trial of the substantive issue as to what, if any, money is owed by the appellants to the respondents.

[4]               We agree with Gillese J.A. that the order challenged is interlocutory in nature because “the action and the merits of the case remain to be determined”. Accordingly, the motion to set aside or vary the order of Gillese J.A.  is dismissed.

[5]               Costs are awarded to the respondents fixed in the total amount of $1,500, inclusive of disbursements and GST.
