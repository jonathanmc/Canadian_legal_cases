MOTION RECORD ENDORSEMENT

[1]               The appellant applies for bail pending his appeal on sexual offences, breach of probation and assault.  The appellant has been refused legal aid.  The appellant was originally granted bail on these charges pending trial but that bail order was revoked when he breached one of the conditions.  He thus spent 265 days in pre-trial custody.  The appellant offered an explanation for that violation.  While the appellant has a history of breaching court orders, he has always appeared for court and I am reasonably satisfied that the appellant would surrender.

[2]               I am also satisfied that the appeal is not frivolous.  The case turned on the credibility of the complainant and the trial judge noted some concerns with her trial evidence because of the possible influence of the mother who had a powerful animus towards the appellant.

[3]               However, I am not satisfied that the appellant’s release is not contrary to the public interest.  The appellant has a long and serious record that includes convictions for crimes of violence and convictions in 1991 for sexual offences.  While the appeal is not frivolous, it is not so strong that this is a case for release.  The trial judge explained why he accepted the complainant’s version of events as disclosed in the video statement that was admitted under s. 715.1 of the Criminal Code.  On the material before me, I see little basis for overturning the conviction.

[4]               Further, Crown counsel advises that the complete transcript is nearly completed and will be completed in August.  In the result the appeal could be heard in September.

[5]               Accordingly, I make the following order:

i)                  The Crown is ordered to obtain the transcript of the trial.

ii)                This appeal is to be argued at the September inmate sittings.

iii)             The application for bail pending appeal is dismissed.  However, the appellant may renew the application for bail if his appeal is not heard in September.
