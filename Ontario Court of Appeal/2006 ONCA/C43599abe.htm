On appeal from the decision of the summary conviction dated October 8, 2003 by Justice Peter A. Cumming of the Superior Court of Justice.

APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]               The respondent concedes that the trial Crown’s cross-examination of the appellant on whether he engaged in money laundering and on his outstanding fraud charges was improper.

[2]               Whether we characterize the improper cross-examination as an error of law or as an error of mixed fact and law, we agree with Cumming J. that it caused no substantial unfairness.

[3]               The appellant denied that he engaged in money laundering and the trial judge specifically instructed herself that the question was not evidence and she would not take it into account.

[4]               To accept that the trial judge relied on the existence of an outstanding fraud charge in assessing the appellant’s credibility would, in effect, mean that she ignored the most fundamental principle of our criminal law: the principle of innocence. This is not a tenable proposition, especially in the light of the trial judge’s extensive and careful reasons for rejecting the appellant’s evidence.

[5]               Accordingly, we are not persuaded that any miscarriage of justice occurred here. The appeal is dismissed.
