ENDORSEMENT

[1]               The respondent purchased two tractor-trailers which, apparently unknown to it, had been stolen.  The respondent added the trailers to its fleet insurance with Zurich Insurance Company and paid the increased premium. 

[2]               Ultimately the tractors were seized by the police, and returned to their rightful owners.  The respondent claimed for the loss of the trailers under its fleet insurance policy. 

[3]               In our view none of the perils listed in the policy provides coverage for this kind of loss.  Even if the loss could be described as a loss due to theft, there is no coverage because the theft occurred before the respondent obtained the insurance for the two trailers. 

[4]               We therefore allow the appeal, and set aside the order of Justice Reilly.  In its place we would:

a)      grant Zurich’s motion for summary judgment and dismiss the action of the respondent against Zurich, and

b)     dismiss the respondents’ motion for summary judgment.

[5]               The appellant is entitled to its costs of the appeal and the costs of the proceedings below fixed in the total amount of $20,000 inclusive of disbursements and G.S.T.
