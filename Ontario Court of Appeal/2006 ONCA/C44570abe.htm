APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]               The letter was sent to the appellant and copied to the Royal College, after the initial complaint and after the appellant was likely told by the College not to contact her.

[2]               The conclusion of the motions judge that the correspondence was incidental or “inextricably connected” to the initial complaint to the Royal College is amply supported by the evidence. Privilege applied.

[3]               The further conclusion that the action was nothing more than an attempt to seek reprisal against the respondent for filing the complaint was available to the motions judge on the evidence and justifies the costs award.

[4]               We see no basis to interfere. The appeal is dismissed with costs fixed at $10,000 all inclusive.
