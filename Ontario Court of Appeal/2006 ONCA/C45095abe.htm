APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]               This was a very serious offence that would normally attract at least a three year sentence.  While the guilty plea was an important mitigating consideration, there were no other factors that would justify a sentence below three years.  Indeed, the element of planning makes this a somewhat more egregious case.

[2]               Three years would have been the appropriate sentence.  At trial, however, the respondent has served nine months in a reformatory and is making progress addressing his addiction problems.  We do not think it would be in the interests of justice to send him to the penitentiary at this stage.  We would, however, impose a maximum reformatory sentence of two years less a day to be followed by three years probation on the time imposed by the trial judge.  The other orders made by the trial judge stand.

[3]               Leave to appeal is granted, the appeal is allowed and the sentence is varied accordingly.
