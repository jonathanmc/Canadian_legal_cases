On appeal from the sentence imposed by Justice Alan D. Cooper of the Ontario Court of Justice, on May 16, 2006.

APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]               The appellant pleaded guilty to charges of indecent act, sexual assault and sexual interference.  The Crown asked for a sentence of 6 to 9 months.  The trial judge imposed a sentence of 18 months.  Yet he gave no reasons for why he so substantially exceeded the Crown’s position.

[2]               In our view, in the circumstance of this case, the failure to give reasons for doubling the high end of the Crown’s request amounted to an error in principle.

[3]               In the light of the appellant’s guilty plea, his age and personal circumstances a sentence of 18 months might well have been outside an acceptable range.

[4]               The appellant has now served seven months of his sentence.  We think that a fit disposition would be to vary the sentence to time served.

[5]               Accordingly, leave to appeal sentence is granted and the sentence is varied to time served.
