APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]               We are not persuaded that there is any basis for interfering with the decision of the motion judge.  We do not agree that she considered the appellant’s third party claim in isolation from the main action.  She weighed all the relevant factors and decided that the third party claim did not have a real and substantial connection to Ontario.  We agree that the considerations regarding a multiplicity of proceedings and a risk of inconsistent results are outweighed by the other factors.  In particular, we note that the appellant did not establish that a Florida court would enforce the judgment of an Ontario court, or that in equivalent circumstances an Ontario court would enforce the judgment of a Florida court.  The unfairness to a defendant in assuming jurisdiction is increased where the courts do not recognize and enforce each other’s judgments.

[2]               The appeal is dismissed.  Costs to the respondent are fixed in the amount of $5,000, inclusive of GST and disbursements.
