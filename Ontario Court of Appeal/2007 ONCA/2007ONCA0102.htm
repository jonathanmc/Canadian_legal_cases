On appeal from the sentence imposed by Justice Peter Tetley of the Ontario Court of Justice dated December 2, 2005

APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]               The appellant abandoned his request for a conditional discharge.  We see no merit in the submission that the trial judge over-emphasized general deterrence and failed to give sufficient weight to various mitigating factors.  This was a deliberate and prolonged course of deceitful conduct that had had serious impact on the victim.  On the fact of this case, the trial judge’s failure to advert to pre-trial bail conditions is not fatal:  see R. v. Laws, [2007] O.J. No. 50 (C.A.).  In any event, this was a fit sentence.  Leave to appeal sentence is granted but the appeal is dismissed.
