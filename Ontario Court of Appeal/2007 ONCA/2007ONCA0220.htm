ENDORSEMENT

[1]               It would have been preferable had the trial judge dealt explicitly with the medical evidence offered in mitigation of sentence, the fact that the appellant was a first offender and the fact that she had pleaded guilty.  However, reading his reasons as a whole and considering the sentence he imposed, we are not persuaded that he failed to take these factors into account.

[2]               This was a vicious attack from which the victim narrowly escaped death and suffered permanent injury.  A sentence of five years is well within the range for an offender with a personal and medical history of the appellant.  We note as well that the appellant is now on full parole and that had she been given a reformatory sentence as requested, that sentence would almost certainly been accompanied by a lengthy period of probation.

[3]               For these reasons, we grant leave to appeal sentence but dismiss the appeal.
