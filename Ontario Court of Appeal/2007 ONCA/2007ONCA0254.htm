On appeal from the conviction entered by Justice Bruce J. Young of the Ontario Court of Justice dated January 16, 2006 and from the sentence imposed by Justice Young on February 15, 2006.

ENDORSEMENT

[1]               In our view the reasons of the trial judge are entirely sufficient for proper appellate review.  He concluded that the appellant’s story that he was invited to the apartment of a stranger he had just met on the street to engage in consensual sex simply, as the Crown argued, made no sense.  Nor did it raise a reasonable doubt.  Rather, he concluded that the Crown had sufficiently established the case for conviction.

[2]               We also see no error of law in those reasons.  The trial judge did not reject the appellant’s evidence because he believed the complainant.  While it would have been preferable had the trial judge not said that the complainant’s story is to be preferred to the appellant’s, it is clear that by that point in his reasons, he had already rejected the appellant’s evidence. 

[3]               In the end the trial judge properly stated and applied R. v. W.D.  He did not reverse the burden of proof. 

[4]               The appeal must therefore be dismissed.
