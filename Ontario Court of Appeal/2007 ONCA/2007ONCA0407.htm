ENDORSEMENT

[1]               The fresh evidence changes the complexion of this matter. It lends support to the applicant’s contention that the deceased was the aggressor and that the stabbing, which consisted of a single stab wound to the deceased’s stomach, may have been in response to the aggression. To that extent, it provides some explanation for what the learned application judge described as “inexplicable” conduct and behaviour that was “bold and brazen”.

[2]               In light of this new information, we do not believe that the public would lose faith in the administration of justice if bail were granted.

[3]               On the secondary ground, we believe that the application judge failed to give adequate consideration to the ability of the sureties and other concerned family members to monitor the applicant’s conduct and prevent him for committing further crimes. We are satisfied that with the appropriate conditions, there is no substantial likelihood that the applicant will commit further offences and thus, he has met his onus on the secondary ground.

[4]               Accordingly, an order will go releasing the applicant on bail pending trial on terms and conditions put forward by the parties and approved by the court. The non-publication order will continue.
