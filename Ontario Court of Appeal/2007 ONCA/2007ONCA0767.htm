APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]               The respondent concedes and we agree, that the learned Appeal Judge erred in holding that notice and an opportunity to comply is a precondition for the enforcement of a zoning by-law by way of prosecution.

[2]               That said, the respondent seeks to support the decision of the appeal judge, acquitting his client, on other grounds.

[3]               Assuming, without deciding, that we have jurisdiction to consider those grounds, we would not give effect to them.

[4]               The respondent did not argue officially induced error below and in any event, we are not satisfied that that defence is made out on this record.

[5]               We see no error in the treatment of the expert evidence by the Justice of the Peace; nor are we persuaded that he erred in concluding that the unit was designed for use as single house-keeping establishment. The later two issues, in our view, are largely fact driven and we see no basis for interfering with the findings of the Justice of the Peace.

[6]               Accordingly, the appeal is allowed and the conviction is restored.
