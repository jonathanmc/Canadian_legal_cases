APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]               We agree with the appellant that the trial judge took into account inadmissible hearsay evidence in arriving at his verdict. Accordingly, the convictions cannot stand.

[2]               We disagree with the appellant that on the basis of the evidence that was admissible, no reasonable jury properly instructed could convict. Applying the test for unreasonable verdict in R. v. Biniaris, we are of the view that the identification evidence, though perhaps weak, was nonetheless sufficient to sustain a conviction. The identifying officer had a good look at the appellant from twelve feet away and then from three or four feet away, and the next day, as part of his investigation, he viewed a photograph of the appellant and there was instantaneous recognition. Although these were some discrepancies between the officer’s original observation and the appellant’s actual appearance, having regard to the photo identification, we do not see them as being so significant as to lead us to conclude that the verdict is unreasonable.

[3]               Accordingly, the appeal is allowed, the convictions are set aside and a new trial is ordered.
