APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]               The trial judge was entitled as the trier of fact to draw the inculpatory inference he did from the appellants response when confronted by the victims mother when he was found in the locked bathroom with the victim.

[2]               The trial judge did not give detailed consideration to the complainants evidence as it related to the allegations of historical assaults.  He did, however, give full and careful consideration to the allegations relating to the April 11 event.  His findings that the complainant was entirely credible on that allegation and that the allegation was proved beyond a reasonable doubt are unassailable.

[3]               The findings with respect to the April 11 event, in the circumstances of this case, provided powerful support for the historical allegations.  Her failure to give detailed separate consideration to the complainants evidence relating to the historical events does not constitute error.  The appeal is dismissed.

[4]               While the sentence is at the high end of the appropriate range, we cannot say it is outside of the range.  We also find no error in principle.  Leave to appeal sentence is granted and the appeal is dismissed.
