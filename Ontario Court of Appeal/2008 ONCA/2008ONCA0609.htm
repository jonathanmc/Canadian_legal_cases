ENDORSEMENT

[1]               The appellant appeals the sentence of two years and nine months imprisonment imposed for sexual assault. The appellant submits the sentence is unfit because the trial judge placed undue emphasis on the appellant’s position of trust and authority. He argues that the appellant was convicted as a fraudster not a genuine healer and should not be sentenced as if he was a real doctor. We do not agree. The appellant not only held himself out as a healer, but insisted throughout the trial and the sentence proceedings that he was a religious consultant and genuine healer and could remove black magic. He used this position to fraudulently obtain the victim’s consent for sexual acts, including sexual intercourse.

[2]               The appellant also submits that the sentence is disproportionate given the  immigration consequences. In our view, the sentence was fit and this is not the kind of case where the sentence should be adjusted because of the immigration consequences.

[3]               Accordingly, while leave to appeal sentence is granted, the appeal is dismissed.
