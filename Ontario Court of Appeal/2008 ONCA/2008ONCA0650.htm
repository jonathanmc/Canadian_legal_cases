APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]               The sole issue in this appeal is the reasonableness of the trial judges finding that the appellant had anal intercourse with the victim.  The appellant does not argue that the trial judge erred in finding that if there was anal intercourse the victim did not consent.

[2]               We see no basis to interfere with the trial judges finding.

[3]               The appellant admitted to the victim that he had anal intercourse with her at a time when she was extremely intoxicated.  It is conceded that the appellants admission was properly admitted into evidence. 

[4]               The appellant did not provide an innocent explanation for his admission.  This court is entitled to take his failure to do so into account in assessing the reasonableness of the verdict.

[5]               In addition there was some evidence that tended to corroborate the appellants admission including the subsequent soreness experienced by the victim.

[6]               In our view there was ample evidence to support the trial judges finding that the appellant had anal intercourse with the victim.  The appeal is dismissed.
