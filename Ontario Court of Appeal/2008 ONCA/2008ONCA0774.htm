APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]               This case turns on the application of s. 5(1)(b) of The Limitations Act, 2002.  The primary facts are not in issue – there is no credibility contest in this case.  In the circumstances of this case, there was an evidentiary burden on the plaintiff/appellant to establish due diligence.  That burden was not met in this case.  The appeal, therefore, is dismissed.

[2]               The respondents are entitled to their costs in the amount of $5,000, inclusive of disbursements and GST.
