[2]               This case is governed by R. v. Bernshaw (1995), 95 C.C.C. (3d) 193 and R. v. George (2004), 187 C.C.C. (3d) at 289 (Ont. C.A. ) at para. 46. To be clear, when there is a 15 minute delay for the purpose of obtaining a proper breath sample, the demand is valid and the delay is a justified limitation on the right to counsel under s. 10(b). The fact that the detainee or the officer had cell phones is therefore irrelevant.
