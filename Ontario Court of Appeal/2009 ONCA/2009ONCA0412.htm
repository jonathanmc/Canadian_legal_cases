APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]               The reasons demonstrate a full appreciation of the evidence and a careful analysis of the evidence.  The trial judge acknowledged the lies told by the complainant and understood their importance to her assessment of the complainants credibility.  Ultimately, she accepted the complainants evidence and gave reasons for doing so.  Those reasons, in addition to the manner in which the complainant first told her story to the police, provided a basis upon which the trial judge could accept the evidence of the complainant.  We must defer to that finding. 

[2]               In our view, the ruling that the accused (who chose not to testify) could not show his arms to the judge to demonstrate that he had no tattoos as described by the complainant was a matter for the discretion of the trial judge.  Equally, in light of the appellants undertaking to call an officer to give the same evidence, we see no error in that exercise of discretion even though, for reasons not apparent on the record, the defence did not call the evidence.

[3]               The conviction appeal is dismissed.  The sentence appeal is dismissed.
