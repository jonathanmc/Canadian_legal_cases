APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]               The appeal is dismissed.  There was ample evidence to support the trial judge’s finding that the appellant attempted to penetrate the complainant’s anus.  The appellant entered the complainant’s bedroom through a closed door and straddled her as she lay face down on her bed asleep.  She was dressed only in her underpants.  He placed his arms on her shoulders and his hands on her back.  The complainant was awoken by a sharp pain in her anus.  As she rose, her back hit the appellant’s stomach.  She testified that the pain was likely caused by the appellant’s penis.  She was not cross-examined on this part of her evidence.  Taken together, these circumstances support the trial judge’s conclusion.  We see no basis to interfere.
