On appeal from the sentence imposed by Justice J.J. Keaney of the Ontario Court of Justice dated January 26, 2006.

APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]              We are told that the immigration authorities treat this as a 2-year sentence because of the length of the pre-trial custody.  Without in any way endorsing that interpretation, we are prepared, based in part on the Crown’s consent, to reduce the sentence to 53 weeks, resulting in an effective sentence, according to what we are told is the way immigration authorities calculate these matters of 2 years less a week.

[2]              The appeal is allowed.
