ENDORSEMENT

[1]              In our view, this case is very close to R. v. Rice, [2004] O.J. No. 5197.  It is an exceptional case where the trial judge took careful account of all of the relevant factors, including the post-offence treatment of the accused, and the circumstances of the accused, the offence and the community.  The trial judge crafted a sentence that set in place a combination of treatment, incarceration near his community, and strict probation in his community that will last for seven years.  For this 58-year-old native man with an exceptionally difficult background, we cannot say that the sentence is manifestly unfit.

[2]              We add this qualification.  We do not think that 1:1 credit for the time spent in a treatment centre while on bail is appropriate.  The case law does not support credit at this level.  However, as in Rice, this qualification does not lead us to say that the ultimate sentence imposed by the trial judge is unreasonable.

[3]              The appeal is dismissed.
