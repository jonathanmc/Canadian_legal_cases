On appeal from the order of Justice Alison Harvison Young of the Superior Court of Justice dated June 9, 2009 and on a motion to quash the appeal.

ENDORSEMENT

[1]              The motion to quash is granted.  We are not prepared to hear this appeal in the circumstances.  The appellant’s conduct is consistent with the conduct that led to the striking of his pleadings.  He has made no effort to bring the appeal forward and seeks to obstruct and increase the costs to the respondent. 

[2]              He made no response to the motion to quash until showing up in court today where he sought an adjournment, which was declined.  Proof of service showed that he had been properly served in November.  He admitted that he was contacted by the court and advised of the date of the hearing. 

[3]              The grounds of appeal in the notice of appeal are merely fact based.  The reasons of the trial judge were comprehensive and delineate a consistent pattern of delay and obstruction.  On the record before us, the appeal appears to be vexatious and a continuation of the previous pattern.  As a result, we are of the opinion that the appeal is an abuse of process. 

[4]              Accordingly, an order will go quashing the appeal.  Costs of the motion are to the moving party fixed in the amount of $5,000, all inclusive.
