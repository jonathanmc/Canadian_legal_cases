APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]              The appellant appeals from the judgment of Parayeski J. dated 8 October 2009 dismissing his motion for a permanent stay of the Respondent’s crossclaim.  The principal ground of appeal is that there is already an action involving those parties in Nova Scotia and that, therefore, the motion judge erred by not dismissing the crossclaim on the basis of forum non conveniens. 

[2]              We disagree.  Nothing has happened in the Nova Scotia action for almost two years.  Moreover, the Ontario action involves all of the relevant parties – the appellant, the respondent, the Bank of Montreal and the Canada Revenue Agency.  Importantly, the Ontario action was initiated by the Bank of Montreal with the Respondent as one of two defendants.  As recognized by the motion judge, the Respondent’s crossclaim against his co-defendant is a natural and appropriate step for him to take.  In addition, as stated by the motion judge, granting a stay would deprive the respondent of a juridical advantage.

[3]              The appeal is dismissed.

[4]              The respondent shall have his costs fixed in the amount of $6,500 all inclusive as agreed between counsel.
