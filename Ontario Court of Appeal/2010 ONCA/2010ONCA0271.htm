ENDORSEMENT

[1]              Mr. Smith brought an action against Mr. Hayden for copyright infringement.  He alleged that Mr. Hayden infringed copyright in one of his drawings by creating, in 1974, a sculpture entitled “All Things Being Equal”.  Mr. Smith maintains that the sculpture is based on his designs, which he describes as representing an “arrangement of an infinite number of polygons.”

[2]              The trial judge dismissed the action on a number of bases.  Mr. Smith appeals.

[3]              Of the numerous grounds of appeal advanced, we find it necessary to deal only with one, as it is determinative.

[4]              The trial judge found that Mr. Smith knew of the Hayden sculpture as early as the late 1980’s, some 15 years or more prior to commencing the action.  The trial judge’s finding rested, in part, on the evidence of Mr. Sowdon, a friend of both parties.  There is no basis for interfering with this finding of the trial judge.

[5]              Consequently, even if the alleged infringement took place, the action was time barred pursuant to s. 41 of the Copyright Act.

[6]              Section 41(1) of the Copyright Act prevents a court from awarding a remedy in relation to an infringement unless proceedings for the infringement are commenced within three years after the infringement occurred, or three years from the time the plaintiff knew or could reasonably have been expected to know of the infringement.

[7]              We conclude by noting that the trial judge provided thorough, thoughtful reasons for the various conclusions he reached.  Those conclusions necessarily depended on credibility findings.  We see no basis on which to interfere with the trial judge’s determinations and conclusions.

[8]              Accordingly, the appeal is dismissed with costs to the respondent fixed at $5,000, inclusive of disbursements and GST.
