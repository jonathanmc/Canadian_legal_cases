APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]              We see no basis for interfering with the trial judge’s findings on liability as regards both appellants.

[2]              We are however of the view that in the circumstances, the 25%  apportionment to the respondent was manifestly low in light of his own degree of responsibility, having particular regard to his high degree of inebriation and a commonplace situation of obvious peril. In our view, apportionment of liability here should have been on a 50/50 basis.

[3]              On the damages question, we see no basis to interfere with any of the trial judge’s calculations, except one, i.e. his calculation for future loss. On that issue, there was simply nothing in the record, either medical or financial, that would justify any award in this category. Accordingly, we would set that aspect of the award aside.

[4]              In the result, the total damage award should be $87,012.94, for which the appellants are 50% responsible, on a joint and several basis.

[5]              Success is mixed. The parties shall bear their own costs of the appeal.
