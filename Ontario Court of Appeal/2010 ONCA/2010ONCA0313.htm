ENDORSEMENT

[1]              The appellant raises two grounds of appeal in relation to the motion judge’s Rule 21 order:

(1)    that the question was hypothetical and contingent upon disputed facts and;

(2)  that the question raised a novel point of law that should be decided on a full record.

[2]              We reject both grounds.

[3]              In our view, when properly read, the order and reasons of the motion judge do not rest upon hypothetical or contingent facts nor do they dispose of any novel point of law.  We read the order and the reasons as determining that the policy covers the cost of repairs of the fire damage including any increase in those costs as a result of building code or bylaw requirements and that exclusion clause (o) has no application to such repairs.  In our view, the motion judge was entitled to make that Rule 21 disposition on the basis of the record before him.  The point of law had been dealt with by earlier decisions and was not novel.

[4]              Accordingly, the appeal is dismissed.  Costs to the respondent fixed at $5,400 plus disbursements of $125.52 plus G.S.T.
