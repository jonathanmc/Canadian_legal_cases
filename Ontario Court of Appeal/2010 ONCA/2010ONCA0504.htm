APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]             Having regard to the fact that the appellant pushed a storeowner while brandishing a knife during the course of a convenience store robbery, we are not persuaded that a conditional sentence was available on the facts of this case. In our view, such conduct clearly involved the use of violence and as well endangered the life and safety of the storeowner and therefore falls within two branches of the definition of serious personal injury offence: see R. v. Lebar, 2010 ONCA 220.

[2]              Nor do we agree that the trial judge erred in principle by failing to give sufficient emphasis to the sentencing factor of rehabilitation having regard to the appellant's youth and status as a first offender. The trial judge indicated that but for these factors the sentence imposed would be measured in years as opposed to twelve months. In our opinion, the sentence imposed reflects no error in principle.

[3]              We are told that the appellant is continuing the efforts at rehabilitation he had commenced prior to sentencing and we commend him and encourage him in these efforts. However, a convenience store robbery is an extremely serious offence and we are not persuaded the sentence imposed is manifestly unfit.

[4]              Leave to appeal is granted, but the appeal is dismissed.
