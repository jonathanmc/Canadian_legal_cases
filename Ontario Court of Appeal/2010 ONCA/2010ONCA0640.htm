APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]              The appellant raises several grounds of appeal, all of which essentially relate to the adequacy of the trial judge’s consideration of the evidence and reasons for conviction.  While the reasons are brief and while the trial judge might have elaborated certain points, we are not persuaded that they fail to provide an adequate basis for conviction or reveal any error that would justify appellate intervention.

[2]              This was a short “he said – she said” trial and the issues were clear and straightforward.  The trial judge explained why he believed the complainant and why he rejected the evidence of the appellant.  A trial judge is not required to mention every item of evidence or every possible inconsistency.  It was apparent that the complainant and the appellant had a volatile and at times violent relationship and that at times the complainant had been the aggressor.  The trial judge specifically adverted to R. v. W.D., and acquitted the appellant on one count because he had a reasonable doubt.

[3]              We see no error that would justify this court in setting aside the conviction.  The appeal from conviction is dismissed.

[4]              Given the appellant’s record and the severity of the assaults, we see no basis to interfere with the custodial sentence imposed.  Leave to appeal sentence is granted but the appeal is dismissed.
