APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]              We see no merit in the appeal from conviction.  The trial judges instructions on the approach the jury should take in assessing credibility disclosed no error.  They were in accordance with the principles enunciated by the Supreme Court of Canada in R. v. W. (R.) (1992), 74 C.C.C. (3d.) 134.

[2]              With respect to sentence, while we agree that the total sentence of fourteen and a half years, less 4 years for time spent in pre-trial custody, was severe, we are not persuaded that it was manifestly excessive.  The appellant subjected his entire family to degrading and controlling behaviour on a constant and persistent basis for many years.  In the course of this conduct, he sexually assaulted his two daughters and in doing so, he committed a gross breach of trust.  In the case of one daughter, who was cognitively impaired, the abuse occurred on almost a daily basis between the ages of 5 and 13 and included vaginal and anal intercourse, as well as the use of instruments such as candlesticks and beer bottles.  Both daughters have suffered greatly from the abuse and each has experienced severe psychological and emotional trauma.  In addition, the evidence establishes that the appellant is a pedophile who refuses to recognize his illness.  As such, he constitutes, at least potentially, a continuing risk to other young children.

[3]              On top of these aggravating factors, the appellant attempted to obstruct justice by threatening his wife to change her evidence and by breaching on two occasions his undertaking not to communicate with her.

[4]              In all of the circumstances, as indicated, a total sentence of 14 1/2 years was not, in our view, manifestly excessive.  As the trial judge quite properly observed, there were no mitigating factors that might otherwise have alleviated the severity of the punishment.

[5]              In the result, leave to appeal sentence is granted but the appeal is dismissed, save as regards the sentence imposed on count 9 of the Indictment.  On that count, the trial judge imposed a concurrent sentence of 9 years.  That was an illegal sentence for the offence of assault and we would accordingly vary it to one year concurrent.  In all other respects, the sentence imposed shall remain the same.
