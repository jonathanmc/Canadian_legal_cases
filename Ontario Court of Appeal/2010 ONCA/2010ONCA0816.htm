APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]              Aspects of the Crown’s cross-examination were improper and created a real risk of a miscarriage of justice.  Having regard to the totality of the circumstances, we are satisfied that the cross-examination did not have that effect in this case.

[2]              The “Vetrovec” warning was adequate.  While the points made on appeal could have been included in the instruction and probably would have been had they been requested, they were not essential to a proper “Vetrovec” charge in this case.

[3]              The appeal is dismissed.
