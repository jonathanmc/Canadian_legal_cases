APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]              The trial judge listed several circumstances giving rise to an irresistible inference that the appellant’s driving amounted to dangerous driving. At the conclusion of that list, he referred to the fact that he was satisfied on a balance of probabilities that some of the defendant’s driving skills were impaired by use of marijuana. This statement was no more than an explanation as to why the appellant drove in a dangerous manner. It was not essential to the trial judge’s conclusion that the appellant’s driving constituted a marked departure from the standard of care a reasonable person would exercise in the circumstances. This conclusion is sufficient to dispose of the appeal. It is therefore unnecessary that we deal with the search warrant issue.

[2]              Accordingly, the appeal is dismissed.
