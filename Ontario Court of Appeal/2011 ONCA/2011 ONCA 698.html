APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]              We need not decide whether on the facts found by the trial judge the appellant’s act would also amount to theft by conversion per R. v. Milne (1992), 70 C.C.C. (3d) 481 (S.C.C.). We are satisfied that even if this conduct amounted to theft, the offence of fraud as defined by the Supreme Court of Canada in R. v. Zlatic (1993), 79 C.C.C. (3d) 466 (S.C.C.) was made out. The act of making use of funds which the appellant knew were not his because of wilful blindness as to their source is properly stigmatized as dishonest: see Zlatic at p. 478.

[2]              Accordingly, the appeal is dismissed.
