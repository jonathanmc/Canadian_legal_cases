APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]          We recognize that the Board’s disposition is more restrictive of Mr. Kelly’s liberty than previous Board orders.  However, we are satisfied that there was a solid evidentiary basis for the disposition and that therefore, it is not unreasonable.

[2]          The Board’s reasons show that it took into account the possibility Mr. Kelly might make progress during the years after its disposition by specifically adverting to the possibility of an earlier hearing.

[3]          The Board no doubt rejected the hybrid order proposed by amicus because it wanted to maintain oversight over Mr. Kelly’s disposition.

[4]          The appeal is dismissed.
