APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]          The reasons are adequate as per R. v. Dinardo.  They clearly identify the basis upon which the trial judge convicted.

[2]          We do not agree that the trial judge misapprehended the evidence.  His summary of the complainants evidence in relation to MSN was available on the evidence.

[3]          We see no error in his reference to at least one instance in referring to the allegation of the complainant.

[4]          The trial judge was entitled to determine the effect, if any, of the inconsistencies in the complainants evidence.  He was not obligated to conclude that the two inconsistencies relied on by the appellant were significant.

[5]          The sentence is not unfit.  The trial judge did consider the impact of the bail provisions and we see no reason to disagree with his assessment.

[6]          The appeal is dismissed.
