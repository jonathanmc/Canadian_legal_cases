APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]          Although the Board did not expressly say that the conditions it imposed were the least onerous and restrictive, we are satisfied that it properly applied this criterion.  We note that these conditions were less restrictive of Mr. Potter’s liberty than the conditions in his previous disposition. 

[2]          In substance, the Board concluded that it was premature to provide the condition of supervised accommodation Mr. Potter seeks.  In the light of Dr. Ahmed’s evidence at the hearing, we are not persuaded that the Board’s decision was unreasonable. 

[3]          We add that if Mr. Potter has continued to progress as he says he has, either he or the hospital may seek an early review.  The appeal is dismissed.
