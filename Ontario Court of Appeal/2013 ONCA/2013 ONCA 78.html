APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT


[1]          We agree with the conclusion of the motion judge. It was entirely open to him to conclude that the appellant had not established any debt owed to him by the debtor company. All there was was a book entry made several years after the fact and there was no evidence of any business purpose for the entry whatsoever. Moreover, the debt from the appellant to the debtor company on the other hand, was admitted. No offset was proven.

[2]          Nor was it an error for the motion judge to order direct payment to the respondent rather than the sheriff. There was evidence that the respondent was the only execution creditor. The appeal is dismissed. Costs to the respondent fixed at $15,000 all inclusive.
