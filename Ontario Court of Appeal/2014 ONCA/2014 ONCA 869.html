APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]          We are in substantial agreement with reasons of the motion judge.

[2]          The three-pronged test for adverse possession is well recognized.

[3]          The third requirement, “effective exclusion of the true owner”, applies even in cases of mutual mistake: Shennan v. Szewezyk, [2010] O.J. No. 4426.

[4]          In this case, there were many facts in dispute.  However, on the record, the facts relevant to the third requirement could be determined on the motion.  The motion judge did so at paras. 55-58.

[5]          The appeal is dismissed.

[6]          We agree that the respondents were entitled to substantial indemnity costs.  However, the trial judge awarded full indemnity rather than substantial indemnity.  We would, therefore, reduce the costs to $50,000.

[7]          Costs of the appeal to the respondents fixed at $10,000.
