APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]          The appellant submits that the trial judge erred in admitting and using the similar fact evidence because he placed too much reliance on generic similarities. We do not agree. The trial judge relied on specific similarities as he outlined at para. 32 of his reasons. Moreover, we agree with para. 4 of the Crown’s factum. The trial judge properly applied the law on the admissibility and use of discreditable conduct evidence. This discretionary determination is entitled deference on appeal.

[2]          This appeal is dismissed.
