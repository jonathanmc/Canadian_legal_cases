APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]          The appellant appeals from his conviction for sexual assault.  He submits that the trial judge’s verdict is unreasonable and that the trial judge erred by beginning with a presumption that the appellant was guilty, and by glossing over improbabilities and inconsistencies in the complainant’s evidence.

[2]          We do not accept these submissions.  The only witnesses who testified at trial were the complainant and the complainant's mother.  Read fairly, rather than a presumption that the appellant was guilty, the trial judge's reasons reflect a response to the issues raised by the appellant at trial, as well as an overall assessment of the evidence.  In his reasons, the trial judge addressed substantially all the concerns relating to the evidence the appellant raises on appeal.  In our view, the appellant's arguments are no more than an effort to have us retry the case. That is not our function.

[3]          The appeal is dismissed.
