APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]          We see no basis on which to interfere with the motion judge’s discretionary decision to extend the limitation period under s. 38(3) of the Trustee Act and permit the respondents to add the appellants as named defendants in the action.  When read as a whole, the motion judge’s reasons reveal he found special circumstances were present.  In reaching this conclusion, the motion judge considered the lengthy unexplained delay but was satisfied that it was outweighed by other factors.  It is undisputed that there was no actual prejudice.

[2]          The appeal is dismissed.  In the circumstances, it is unnecessary that we address the cross-appeal.

[3]          Costs of the appeal are to the respondents on a partial indemnity scale fixed in the amount of $10,000 inclusive of disbursements and applicable taxes.
