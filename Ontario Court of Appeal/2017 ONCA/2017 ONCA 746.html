REASONS FOR DECISION

[1]            The Crown appeals from the sentence imposed on two young persons on the ground that it was manifestly unfit. The Crown also asks this Court to set judicial criteria for the grant of a reprimand by a youth court judge. We decline to do so.

[2]            We disagree that the sentence was manifestly unfit. It was a legally available sentence and the sentencing judge considered all relevant factors including the seriousness of the offence and the circumstances of the offenders.

[3]            Both offenders had taken substantial rehabilitative measures on their own. There is no basis to interfere with the trial judge’s exercise of discretion in this case. There will be a publication ban in respect of any information which serves to identify either young person.

[4]            The appeal is dismissed.
