APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT


[1]          The appellant’s principal argument, through duty counsel, is that the trial judge did not deal properly with the complainant’s credibility. He points to what he labels as major inconsistences in three areas of the complainant’s testimony and the corroborating evidence relating to this testimony and says that they do not support the trial judge’s conclusion that the complainant was “truthful and honest with the court”.

[2]          We are not persuaded by this submission. On each of the three points raised by the appellant - the physical examination of the complainant at the hospital, the appellant’s flight from the hotel coupled with the complainant’s simultaneous calls for help, and complainant’s initial hesitation to say that the incident that led to her injury flowed from her work as a prostitute - the trial judge was entitled to find that they corroborated the core of complainant’s evidence. Importantly, the trial judge did not exaggerate the nature or significance of the corroborative evidence.

[3]          The appellant, on his own behalf, asserts that the trial judge erred by finding that he was not a credible witness. We see no basis for criticising the trial judge’s analysis and conclusion on this issue.

[4]          The appeal is dismissed. The sentence appeal is dismissed as abandoned.
