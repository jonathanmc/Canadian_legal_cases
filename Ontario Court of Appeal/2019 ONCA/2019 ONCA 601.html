APPEAL BOOK ENDORSEMENT

[1]          The appellant says that the trial judge erred in two respects: (a) misusing the fact that the appellant was bound by a prohibition order; and (b) finding that the complainant’s credibility was enhanced on the basis that she testified in a way that was not always favourable to her when all of those factors were provable by text messages.

[2]          We do not agree.

[3]          On the first point, the trial judge properly instructed herself on bad character evidence and the need to avoid all propensity reasoning. We see nothing in the reasons to suggest that she failed to adhere to that self-instruction.

[4]          On the second point, it was open to the trial judge to take into account the complainant’s acknowledgements, whether they were in text messages or not.

[5]          At its core, this appeal constitutes a complainant about credibility findings. We would defer to the trial judge’s assessment of credibility.

[6]          On consent of the Crown, the victim fine surcharge will be set aside: R. v. Boudreault, 2018 SCC 58. No other argument was pursued on the sentence appeal.

[7]          The conviction appeal is dismissed. Sentence appeal is dismissed except to the extent of setting aside the victim fine surcharge.
