                                         REASONS FOR DECISION     

[1]          The appellant was convicted of criminal harassment. He appealed to the Superior Court of Justice. This is an application for leave to appeal from a decision of Roberts J., dismissing the summary conviction appeal.

[2]          The appellant appears to wish to re-argue the case that has already been determined. The threshold test for leave to appeal is high: R. v. R.R., 2008 ONCA 497. We see no issue significant to the administration of justice beyond this case and there is no merit to the proposed appeal.

[3]          There is a fresh evidence application before the court involving the translation of certain documents. The fresh evidence merely assists the court in understanding some of the documents, but does not have any bearing on the leave application.

[4]          For these reasons, the fresh evidence application is dismissed and leave to appeal is denied.
